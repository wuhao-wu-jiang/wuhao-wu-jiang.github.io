The Volume of Hamming Ball

Posted on 2021-04-02 Edited on 2021-07-29

Δ(x,y)≐∣{i∈[n]:xi≠yi}∣. \Delta(x, y) \doteq | \{ i \in [n] : x_i \neq y_i \} |. Δ(x,y)≐∣{i∈[n]:xi​​=yi​}∣.

Δ(C)≐min⁡x≠y∈CΔ(x,y). \Delta(C) \doteq \min_{x \neq y \in C} \Delta(x, y). Δ(C)≐x​=y∈Cmin​Δ(x,y).

Entropy

We rely on sub-additivity of entropy.

Let XXX and YYY be discrete random variables, then

  1. H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y)H( X, Y ) \le H(X) + H(Y)H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y).
  2. H(X∣Y)≤H(X)H(X | Y) \le H(X)H(X∣Y)≤H(X).

Proof.
Consider the ball B(1n,r)\mathcal{B}(1^n, r)B(1n,r), and a uniform random point X=(X1,...,Xn)X = (X_1, ..., X_n)X=(X1​,...,Xn​) in the ball B(1n,r)\mathcal{B}(1^n, r)B(1n,r). Its entropy is

H(X)≤H(X1)+H(X2)+...+H(Xn)=nH(Y). \begin{aligned} H(X) &\le H(X_1) + H(X_2) + ... + H(X_n) &= n H(Y). \end{aligned} H(X)​≤H(X1​)+H(X2​)+...+H(Xn​)​=nH(Y).​
