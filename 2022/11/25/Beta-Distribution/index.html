We start with a possibly biased coin which lands on head with unknown probability ppp when tossed.
Suppose we obtain Y=aY = aY=a heads and Z=bZ = bZ=b tails out of a+ba + ba+b tosses.
Conditioned on p=xp = xp=x for some x∈(0,1)x \in (0, 1)x∈(0,1), we have

Pr⁡[Y=a,Z=b∣p=x]=(a+ba)⋅xa(1−x)b. \Pr[ Y = a, Z = b \mid p = x ] = \binom{a + b}{a} \cdot x^a (1 - x)^b. Pr[Y=a,Z=b∣p=x]=(aa+b​)⋅xa(1−x)b.
