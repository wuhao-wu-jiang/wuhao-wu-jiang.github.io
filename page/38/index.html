Gradient Descent

Posted on 2020-03-06 Edited on 2021-09-17

Problem

Gradient descent is a greedy search method for optimization, whose analysis varies depending on the kind of constraints you place on it.

We study a baby version of the method.
Let fff be a twice continuous differentiable and convex function.
We are interested in the minimization problem:

min⁡x∈Rnf(x)\min_{x \in \mathbb{R }^n} f(x) x∈Rnmin​f(x)

The convexity of fff guarantees the existence of a unique minimum value min⁡x∈Rnf(x)\min_{x \in \mathbb{R }^n} f(x)minx∈Rn​f(x).

In addition, we assume that fff indeed achieves this minimum value at some point, denoted as x∗x^*x∗.
We need this assumption, as in some case, fff can never achieve the minimum value.
For example, let f(x)=exf(x) = e^xf(x)=ex.
fff approaches 000 when x→−∞x \rightarrow -\inftyx→−∞ but never achieves it.

Notations

Before we delve into the details, we need to define some frequently used notations.

  • The gradient ∇f(x):Rn→Rn\nabla f(x):\mathbb{R }^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R }^n∇f(x):Rn→Rn is the vector of partial derivatives of fff at point xxx:

∇f(x)=[∂f(x)∂x1,∂f(x)∂x2,..,∂f(x)∂xn]\nabla f(x) = \left[ \frac{\partial f (x) }{\partial x_1}, \frac{\partial f (x)}{\partial x_2}, .., \frac{\partial f (x) }{\partial x_n} \right] ∇f(x)=[∂x1​∂f(x)​,∂x2​∂f(x)​,..,∂xn​∂f(x)​]

  • The Hessian ∇2f(x):Rn→Rn×n\nabla^2 f(x) : \mathbb{R }^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R }^{n \times n}∇2f(x):Rn→Rn×n is the matrix of second order derivatives of fff at xxx:

    ∇f(x)=[∂2f(x)∂x1∂x1,∂2f(x)∂x1∂x2,...,∂2f(x)∂x1∂xn∂2f(x)∂x2∂x1,∂2f(x)∂x2∂x2,...,∂2f(x)∂x2∂xn...∂2f(x)∂xn∂x1,∂2f(x)∂xn∂x2,...,∂2f(x)∂xn∂xn]\nabla f(x) = \left[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_1 \partial x_1}, \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2}, &..., \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_1 \partial x_n} \\ \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1}, \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_2 \partial x_2}, &..., \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\ &...\\ \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_n \partial x_1}, \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_n \partial x_2}, &..., \frac{\partial^2 f (x)}{\partial x_n \partial x_n} \\ \end{aligned} \right] ∇f(x)=⎣⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎡​∂x1​∂x1​∂2f(x)​,∂x1​∂x2​∂2f(x)​,∂x2​∂x1​∂2f(x)​,∂x2​∂x2​∂2f(x)​,∂xn​∂x1​∂2f(x)​,∂xn​∂x2​∂2f(x)​,​...,∂x1​∂xn​∂2f(x)​...,∂x2​∂xn​∂2f(x)​......,∂xn​∂xn​∂2f(x)​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎤​

    Since fff is twice continuous differentiable, the Hessian is symmetric.

Convergence

To analyze the convergence rate of fff, we need additional assumptions.
Here we adopt two strong ones:

Assumptions.

  1. fff is β\betaβ smooth, i.e., ∇f(z)≤βI\nabla f(z) \le \beta I∇f(z)≤βI for ∀z∈Rn\forall z \in \mathbb{R }^n∀z∈Rn.
  2. fff is α\alphaα strongly convex, i.e., ∇f(z)≥αI\nabla f(z) \ge \alpha I∇f(z)≥αI for ∀z∈Rn\forall z \in \mathbb{R }^n∀z∈Rn.

β\betaβ upper bounds the largest possible eigenvalue of the Hessian of fff at each point in R\mathbb{R}R, and α\alphaα lower bounds it.
By definition we have β≥α\beta \ge \alphaβ≥α.

Definitions.

  1. For each t∈[T]t \in [T]t∈[T], let Δt≐f(xt)−f(x∗)\Delta_t \doteq f(x^t) - f(x^*)Δt​≐f(xt)−f(x∗) be the gap between f(xt)f(x^t)f(xt) and the minimum value f(x∗)f(x^*)f(x∗).
  2. Let κ≐βα\kappa \doteq \frac{\beta}{\alpha}κ≐αβ​ between β\betaβ and α\alphaα.
