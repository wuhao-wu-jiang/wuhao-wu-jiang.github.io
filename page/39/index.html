Karger Algorithm

The Karger algorithm was proposed by David R. Karger [Global Min-cuts in RNC, and Other Ramifications of a Simple Min-Cut Algorithm]. It is a randomized algorithm to find a min-cut of an undirected connected graph.

The idea of the algorithm is simple – randomly pick an edge from the graph, and merge the endpoints of this edge into one. The edges incident on two merged endpoints is now incident on the new endpoint. Repeat this process until two nodes are left on the graph. We show that the probability that the number of edges remained between the two nodes equals the value of min-cut is at least 2n(n−1)\frac{2}{n(n - 1)}n(n−1)2​.

Formally, suppose we are given an undirected graph G=(V,E)G = (V, E)G=(V,E) with n=∣V∣n = |V|n=∣V∣ vertices, m=∣E∣m = |E|m=∣E∣ edges, and c∗=c^*=c∗= size of the min-cut.
