Sparse Vector

Posted on 2022-02-08 Edited on 2022-02-12

Let \((\mathcal{X}, \Vert \cdot \Vert)\) be a matrix space.

Definition. Two elements \(D, D' \in \mathcal{X}\) are neighboring, denoted as \(D \sim D'\), if \(\Vert D', D' \Vert = 1\).
Definition. A function \(q: \mathcal{X} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) has sensitivity \(\Delta \in \mathbb{R}^+\), if \[ \Delta = \sup_{ D, D' \in \mathcal{X}, D \sim D' } | q(D) - q(D') |. \]

Problem

Let \(q_i: \mathcal{X} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}, i \in \mathbb{N}^+\) be a sequence of functions with sensitivity \(\Delta\). Let \(T \in \mathbb{R}\) be a threshold. Design an \(\epsilon\)-differentially private algorithm, that reports the first \(i \in \mathbb{N}^+\) for which \(q_i(D)\) is near or above \(T\).

Algorithm

Definition. For each \(b \in \mathbb{R}^+\), let \(\mathcal{Lap}(b)\) be the Laplacian distribution with parameter \(b\).

The algorithm is described as follows.

Algorithm \(\mathcal{A}\).
1. \(\hat T \leftarrow T + Z\) where \(Z \sim \mathcal{Lap}(3 \Delta / \epsilon)\)
2. for \(i \in \mathbb{N}^+\) do
3. \(\qquad\) \(\hat q_i(D) \leftarrow q_i(D) + X_i\), where \(X_i \sim \mathcal{Lap}( 3 \Delta / \epsilon)\)
4. \(\qquad\) if \(\hat q_i(D) \ge \hat T\) then
5. \(\qquad\qquad\) output \(\bot\)
6. \(\qquad\qquad\) halt
7. \(\qquad\) else
8. \(\qquad\qquad\) output \(\top\)

Privacy Guarantee

Let \(D'\) be a neighboring dataset of \(D\).

Theorem. For each \(i \in \mathbb{N}^+\), let \(s_i \doteq \underbrace{\top \ldots \top}_{ i - 1 \text{ occurrences} } \bot\). Then \[ e^{-\epsilon} \le \frac{\Pr[ \mathcal{A}(D) = s_i ]}{\Pr[ \mathcal{A}(D') = s_i ]} \le e^{\epsilon}. \]

Proof. Fix an \(i \in \mathbb{N}^+\). Let \(M(D) = \max_{1 \le j \le i - 1} \Big( q_j(D) + X_j \Big)\). The event \(\mathcal{A}(D) = s_i\) happens if and only if
\[ M(D) < \hat T \le \hat q_i(D). \]

Denote \(\vec X_{i - 1} \doteq (X_1, \ldots, X_{i - 1})\). Fix a sequence \(\vec x_{i - 1} = (x_1, \ldots, x_{i - 1} ) \in \mathbb{R}\). Conditioned on \(\vec X_{i - 1} = \vec x_{i - 1}\), the probability density of \(\Pr\big[ \mathcal{A}(D) = s_i \big]\) is given by \[ \begin{aligned} p\big[ \mathcal{A}(D) = s_i \big] &= p\big[ M(D) < \hat T \le \hat q_i(D) \big] \\ &= p\big[ M(D) < T + Z \le q_i(D) + X_i \big] \\ &= \int \int p_Z(z) \cdot p_{X_i}(x) \cdot \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D) < T + z \le q_i(D) + x \big] \, dx \, dz, \end{aligned} \] where \(p_Z(z)\) and \(p_{X_i}(x)\) are probability densities of \(Z\) and \(X_i\), respectively.

Similarly, the conditional probability density of \(\Pr\big[ \mathcal{A}(D') = s_i \big]\) is given by \[ \begin{aligned} p\big[ \mathcal{A}(D') = s_i \big] &= p\big[ M(D') < \hat T \le \hat q_i(D') \big] \\ &= p\big[ M(D') < T + Z \le q_i(D') + X_i \big] \\ &= \int \int p_Z(z') \cdot p_{X_i}(x') \cdot \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D') < T + z' \le q_i(D') + x' \big] \, dx' \, dz'. \end{aligned} \]

Suppose that the following equations holds: \[ \begin{aligned} T + z' - M(D') &= T + z - M(D), \\ q_i(D') + x' - (T + z') &= q_i(D) + x - (T + z). \end{aligned} \]

Then \[ \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D') < T + z' \le q_i(D') + x' \big] = \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D) < T + z \le q_i(D) + x \big]. \]

But this implies that \[ \begin{aligned} |z' - z| &= |M(D') - M| \le \Delta \\ |x' - x| &= |z' - q_i(D') - (z - q_i(D))| \\ &= |M(D) - M(D') + q_i(D) - q_i(D')| \\ &\le 2 \cdot \Delta. \end{aligned} \]

Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{p\big[ \mathcal{A}(D) = s_i \big]}{p\big[ \mathcal{A}(D') = s_i \big] } &= \frac{ \int \int p_Z(z) \cdot p_{X_i}(x) \cdot \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D) < T + z \le q_i(D) + x \big] \, dx \, dz } { \int \int p_Z(z') \cdot p_{X_i}(x') \cdot \mathbf{1}\big[ M(D') < T + z' \le q_i(D') + x' \big] \, dx' \, dz' } \\ &\le \exp \Big( \Delta \cdot \frac{\epsilon}{3\Delta}\Big) \cdot \exp \Big( 2 \cdot \Delta \cdot \frac{\epsilon}{3\Delta} \Big) \\ &\le \exp(\epsilon). \end{aligned} \]

Integrating over \(\vec X_{i - 1} = \vec x_{i - 1}\) for all possible \(\vec x_{i - 1}\) proves that \(\Pr\big[ \mathcal{A}(D) = s_i \big] \le e^\epsilon \cdot \Pr\big[ \mathcal{A}(D') = s_i \big]\).

\(\square\)

Utility Guarantee

Suppose that the algorithm outputs \(\top\) at the \(i\)-th query, we measure the empirical error by \(\max \{0, q_i(D) - T \}\), i.e., if \(q_i(D)\) does not exceed \(T\), then we regards the empirical error as \(0\). Similarly, for an output \(\bot\), the empirical error is measured by \(\max \{0, T - q_i(D) \}\).

Theorem. Fix a sequence of \(k\) queries \(q_1, \ldots, q_k\) to be performed by the algorithm. With probability at most \(\beta\), the maximum empirical error exceeds \[ \frac{6 \cdot \Delta}{\epsilon}\ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta}. \]

Proof. \[ \Pr \left[ |Z| \ge \frac{2 \cdot \Delta}{\epsilon}\ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta} \right] \le \exp \left( 2 \cdot \ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta} \right) = \frac{\beta}{k + 1}. \] Similarly, for each \(i \in [k]\), \[ \Pr \left[ |X_i| \ge \frac{2 \cdot \Delta}{\epsilon}\ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta} \right] \le \exp \left( 2 \cdot \ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta} \right) = \frac{\beta}{k + 1}. \]

By union bound, with probability at most \(\beta\), it holds that \[ |Z| + \max_{i \in [k]} |X_i| \ge \frac{6 \cdot \Delta}{\epsilon}\ln \frac{k + 1}{\beta}. \]

\(\square\)

Dijkstra's Algorithm With d-Ary Heap

Posted on 2022-02-04

A \(d\)-ary min heap with \(n\) elements has the following time complexity:

  • Insertion. \(O\big((\log n) / (\log d) \big)\).
  • Extract-Min. \(O\big(d \cdot (\log n) / (\log d) \big)\).
  • Decrease-Key. \(O\big((\log n) / (\log d) \big)\).

Recall that for a single source shortest path query on a graph with \(n\) vertices and \(m\) edges, the Dijkstra's Algorithm performs \(n\) extract-min operations, and \(m\) decrease-key operation. Working with \(d\)-ary min heap, it has time complexity \[ O \left( n \cdot d \cdot \frac{\log n}{\log d} + m \cdot \frac{\log n}{\log d} \right) \]

Setting \(d = m / n\) minimizes this to \[ O \left( m \cdot \log_{m / n} n \right) = O \left( m \cdot \frac{\log n}{\log (m / n)} \right). \]

Consider the following cases:

  • \(m = O(n)\), then the time complexity is \(O(n \log n)\).

  • \(m = \Theta (n \log n)\), then the time complexity is \[ O \left( n \cdot (\log n) \cdot \frac{\log n}{\log \log n } \right). \]
    Example:

    1. when \(n = 2^{20} = 1\)M, \((\log n) / (\log \log n) = 20 / (\log 20) \approx 4.6\).
    2. when \(n = 2^{30} = 1\)G, \((\log n) / (\log \log n) = 30 / (\log 20) \approx 6.1\).

  • \(m = \Theta \big( n^{1 + \epsilon} \big)\) for some \(\epsilon > 0\), then the time complexity is \[ O \left( \frac{m}{\epsilon} \right). \]

Total Variance Distance

Posted on 2022-01-31 Edited on 2022-02-02

Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space, \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) be two probability measure.

Definition. The total variance distance between \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) is defined as \[ \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \doteq \sup_{ E \in \mathcal{F} } \big| \mu(E) - \nu(E) \big|. \]

Property. \(\Vert \cdot \Vert_{TV}\) is a metric.

Proof.

  1. Clearly \(\Vert \cdot \Vert_{TV}\) is non-negative.

  2. If \(\Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} = 0\), then \(\mu(E) = \nu(E)\), for each \(E \in \mathcal{F}\). Therefore, \(\mu = \nu\).

  3. Let \(\kappa\) be another probability measure. Then \[ \begin{aligned} \Vert \mu - \kappa \Vert_{TV} &= \sup_{ E \in \mathcal{F} } \big| \mu(E) - \kappa(E) \big| \\ &\le \sup_{ E \in \mathcal{F} } \left( \big| \mu(E) - \nu(E) \big| + \big| \nu(E) - \kappa(E) \big| \right) \\ &\le \sup_{ E \in \mathcal{F} } \big| \mu(E) - \nu(E) \big| + \sup_{ E \in \mathcal{F} } \big| \nu(E) - \kappa(E) \big| \\ &= \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} + \Vert \nu - \kappa \Vert_{TV}. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

Property. \[ \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \doteq \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sup \left\{ \int f \, d \mu - \int f \, d \nu : \Vert f \Vert_\infty \le 1 \right\}. \]

Proof. Since both \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) are probability measure, \(\kappa \doteq \mu - \nu\) is a signed measure. Via Hahn Decomposition,
\[ \exists A \in \mathcal{F}, \quad \text{s.t.,}\, \begin{cases} \kappa(A) = \sup \{ \kappa(E) : E \in \mathcal{F} \}, \\ \kappa(E) \ge 0, &\forall E \subset A \, \wedge\, E \in \mathcal{F}, \\ \kappa(E) \le 0, &\forall E \subset \bar A \, \wedge\, E \in \mathcal{F}. \end{cases} \]

Hence, \(\Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} = \mu(A) - \nu(A) = \kappa(A)\). Let \(f \doteq \mathbf{1}_{A} - \mathbf{1}_{\bar A}\). Then \(\Vert f \Vert_\infty \le 1\), and \[ \begin{aligned} \int f \, d \mu - \int f \, d \nu &= \mu(A) - \nu(A) + \nu(\bar A) - \mu(\bar A) \\ &= 2 \cdot \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)
