Holder's Inequality

Posted on 2022-03-31

Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})\) be a probability space, and \(X, Y: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be random variables.

Holder's Inequality. Let \(p, q \in \mathbb{R}^+\), such that \(1 / p + 1 / q = 1\). Then \[ \mathbb{E}\left[ |X| \cdot |Y| \right] \le \mathbb{E}^{1 / p} \left[ |X|^p \right] \cdot \mathbb{E}^{1 / q} \left[ |Y|^q \right] \]

Remark 1. If \(|X|^p\) and \(|Y|^q\) are integrable, so is \(|X| \cdot |Y|\).

Proof. The claim is trivial, if either \(\mathbb{E}[ |X|^p ] = \infty\) or \(\mathbb{E}[ |Y|^q ] = \infty\).

Therefore, we consider just the case when both \(|X|^p\) and \(|Y|^q\) are integrable.
By concavity of the function \(f(t) = \ln t\): \[ \frac{1}{p} \ln a^p + \frac{1}{q} \ln b^q \le \ln \left( \frac{a^p}{p} + \frac{b^q}{q} \right), \qquad \forall a, b > 0. \] We obtain the Young's Inequality: \[ a b \le \frac{a^p}{p} + \frac{b^q}{q}, \qquad \forall a, b > 0. \]

Case 1. Suppose that both \(X\) and \(Y\) are simple, non-negative random variables. WLOG, assume that \(X = \sum a_i \mathbb{I}_{E_i}\), and \(Y = \sum b_i \mathbb{I}_{E_i}\). Further, define \(s = \mathbb{E}^{1 / p} \left[ |X|^p \right]\) and \(t = \mathbb{E}^{1 / q} \left[ |Y|^q \right]\). We have \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}\left[ \frac{|X|}{s} \cdot \frac{|Y|}{t} \right] &= \sum \frac{a_i}{s} \cdot \frac{b_i}{t} \cdot \mathbb{P}(E_i) \\ &\le \sum \left( \frac{a_i^p}{s^p} + \frac{b_i^p}{t^q}\right) \cdot \mathbb{P}(E_i) \\ &= \frac{1}{s^p} \cdot \mathbb{E} \left[ |X|^p \right] + \frac{1}{t^q} \cdot \mathbb{E} \left[ |Y|^q \right] \\ &= 1. \end{aligned} \]

Case 2. Suppose that both \(X\) and \(Y\) are random variables. Choose random variables \(X_n\) and \(Y_n\) such that \(0 \le X_n \uparrow |X|\), and \(0 \le Y_n \uparrow |Y|\). Following Case 1 we have \[ \mathbb{E}\left[ X_n \cdot Y_n \right] \le \mathbb{E}^{1 / p} \left[ X_n^p \right] \cdot \mathbb{E}^{1 / q} \left[ Y_n^q \right]. \]

Further, it follows by monotone convergence theorem that \[ \lim_n \mathbb{E}\left[ X_n Y_n \right] = \mathbb{E}\left[ |X| \cdot |Y| \right], \] and \[ \begin{aligned} \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ X_n^p \right] = \mathbb{E} \left[ |X|^p \right], \quad \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ Y_n^q \right] = \mathbb{E} \left[ |Y|^q \right]. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

Reference

[1] P. Billingsley, Probability and measure Anniversary Edition, 3rd ed. New York: Wiley, 1995.

Clarkson Type Inequalities

Posted on 2022-03-31

Theorem. Let \(p \in (1, 2]\). Then for \(x, y \in \mathbb{R}\), it holds that \[ \frac{1}{2} |x - y|^p + \frac{1}{2} |x + y|^p \le |x|^p + |y|^p. \]

Proof. Let \(z = \big(a, b\big) \in \mathbb{R}^2\). Since \(p \le 2\), it holds that \[ \left( |a|^p + |b|^p \right)^{1 / p} = \Vert z \Vert_p \ge \Vert z \Vert_2 = \left( |a|^2 + |b|^2 \right)^{1 / 2} = \left( \frac{1}{2}|a + b|^2 + \frac{1}{2}|a - b|^2 \right)^{1 / 2}. \] Raising the power of both hand sides by \(p\), and via concavity of the function \(y = x^{p / 2}\), we get
\[ \begin{aligned} |a|^p + |b|^p &\ge \left( \frac{1}{2}|a + b|^2 + \frac{1}{2}|a - b|^2 \right)^{p / 2} \\ &\ge \frac{1}{2} \left( |a + b|^2 \right)^{p / 2} + \frac{1}{2} \left( |a - b|^2 \right)^{p / 2} \\ &\ge \frac{1}{2} |a + b|^p + \frac{1}{2} |a - b|^p. \end{aligned} \] \(\square\)

Remark: in general, for \(z \in \mathbb{R}^n\), and \(0 < q \le p\), we have \[ \Vert z \Vert_q = \left( \sum_{i \in [n]} |z_i|^q \right)^{1 / q} \ge \Vert z \Vert_p = \left( \sum_{i \in [n]} |z_i|^p \right)^{1 / p} \]

To prove this, let \(y_i = |z_i|^q\) for each \(i \in [n]\), and \(r = p / q \ge 1\). It suffices to show that \[ \left( \sum_{i \in [n]} y_i \right)^r \ge \sum_{i \in [n]} y_i^r. \]

Further, let \(x_j = y_j / \left( \sum_{i \in [n]} y_i \right)\) for each \(j \in [n]\). It reduces to prove that
\[ \sum_{j \in [n]} x_j^r \le \sum_{j \in [n]} x_j =1 . \]

Heavy-Tailed Distribution

Posted on 2022-03-30

Let \((\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \Pr)\) be a probability space, and \(X: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a random variable. Assume that there exists constants \(a \in (0, 1]\), \(b \in \R^+\), such that \[ \mathbb{E}\left[|X|^{1 + a}\right] \le b. \]

Theorem. Let \(T \in \R^+\). Then \[ \mathbb{E}\left[|X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} \right] \le \frac{b}{ T^a}. \]

Proof. \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}\left[|X|^{1 + a}\right] &= \int_0^\infty \Pr\left[ |X|^{1 + a} > r \right] \, dr = \int_0^\infty \Pr\left[ |X|^{1 + a} > s^{1 + a} \right] \, ds^{1 + a} \\ &= \int_0^\infty (1 + a) s^a \cdot \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s \\ &\ge \int_0^T (1 + a) s^a \cdot \Pr\left[ |X| > T \right] \, d s + (1 + a) \cdot T^a \cdot \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s \\ &= T^{1 + a} \cdot \Pr[ |X| > T ] + (1 + a) \cdot T^a \cdot \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s. \end{aligned} \]

Next, observe that for \(s \in [0, T]\), \[ \begin{aligned} \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] &= \Pr\left[ \left\{ \omega \in \Omega : |X(\omega)| > s \wedge |X(\omega)| > T \right\} \right] % &= \Pr\left[ \left\{ \omega \in \Omega : |X(\omega)| > T \right\} \right] \\ = \Pr\left[ |X| > T \right] . \end{aligned} \] and for \(s > T\), \[ \begin{aligned} \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] &= \Pr\left[ \left\{ \omega \in \Omega : |X(\omega)| > s \wedge |X(\omega)| > T \right\} \right] = \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] . \end{aligned} \]

Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}\left[|X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} \right] &= \int_0^T \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] \, d s + \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] \, d s \\ &= T \cdot \Pr[ |X| > T ] + \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] \end{aligned} \]

It concludes that \[ T^a \cdot \mathbb{E}\left[|X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} \right] \le \mathbb{E}\left[|X|^{1 + a}\right] \le b. \]

\(\square\)
