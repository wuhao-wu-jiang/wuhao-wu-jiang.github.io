Posted on 2021-06-15 Edited on 2021-06-16

We study inequalities that play important roles in obtaining Chernoff bounds. These inequalities are related to the following function defined as

h(t)=(1+t)ln⁡(1+t)−t ,∀t>−1. h(t) = (1 + t) \ln (1 + t) - t\,,\qquad \forall t > -1. h(t)=(1+t)ln(1+t)−t,∀t>−1.

h(0)=h′(0)=0 , and h(2+n)(0)=(−1)n⋅n! ,∀n∈N . \begin{aligned} h(0) = h'(0) = 0 \,, \text{ and } \quad h^{ (2 + n) } (0) = (-1)^n \cdot n!\,, \qquad \forall n \in \mathbb{N} \,. \end{aligned} h(0)=h′(0)=0, and h(2+n)(0)=(−1)n⋅n!,∀n∈N.​
