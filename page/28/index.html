Propose-Test-Release

Posted on 2020-11-23 Edited on 2025-08-20

Let \((\mathcal{X}, d)\) be a metric space and \(q : \mathcal{X} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a function defined on \(\mathcal{X}\). The metric \(d: \mathcal{X} \rightarrow \mathbb{N}\) takes only integer values. For an \(x \in \mathcal{X}\), it neighbors an \(x' \in \mathcal{X}\), termed \(x \sim x'\), if \(d(x, x') = 1\).

Further, define the global sensitivity \(\Delta\) of \(q\) as: \[ \Delta \doteq \max_{x \sim x' } | q(x) - q(x') | \]

The Laplace mechanism \(M\) of \(q\) adds random noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\Delta}{\varepsilon})\) to the output of \(q\): \[ M(x) \doteq q(x) + N \]

where \(\mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\Delta}{\varepsilon})\) is the Laplacian distribution with parameter \(\frac{\Delta}{\varepsilon}\). It guarantees that if \(x \sim x'\), the output distributions of \(M(x)\) and \(M(x')\) are indistinguishable in the sense that for all \(S \subset \mathbb{R}\), s.t. \(S\) is measurable, it holds that \[ \Pr[ M(x) \in S] \in [ e^{ - \varepsilon }, e^{ \varepsilon } ] \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \in S] \]

The value of \(\Delta\) could be large and is independent of an \(x \in \mathcal{X}\). We would like to investigate the possibility of adding a smaller noise than \(\mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\Delta}{\varepsilon})\).

For a fixed \(x\), we can define the local sensitivity with respect to \(x\) as \[ \delta(x) \doteq \max_{x' \sim x} | q(x) - q(x') |. \]

Here the maximum is over the neighbors of a fixed \(x\). The value between \(\Delta\) and \(\delta(x)\) is quite different.

Example. Let * \(\mathcal{X} \doteq [0, 100]^3\).
* \(d(x, x')\doteq \#\text{ different values between } x \text{ and } x'\). * \(q(x) \doteq \min_{1 \le i < j \le 3} |x_i - x_j|\), where \(x_i\) is the \(i\)-dimension of \(x\).

Then for the point \(x = (0, 0, 0) \in [0, 100]^3\), if \(x' \sim x\), \(x'\) contains at least two zeros in some of its dimensions. Therefore, \(q(x') = 0\) and \(\delta(x) = 0\). But for \(x = (0, 0, 100)\), it has a neighbor \(x' = (0, 50, 100)\). Now \(q(x) = 0\) but \(q(x') = 50\). So \(\Delta \ge \delta(x) \ge 50\).

We are interested in whether adding smaller noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\delta(x) }{\varepsilon})\) to the output of \(q(x)\) would preserve privacy: \[ M(x) = q(x) + N. \]

However, this approach is not private. In the previous example, \((0, 0, 0)\) and \((0, 0, 100)\) are neighbors. But for the former we add noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(0)\) and for the latter we add noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(\frac{50}{\varepsilon})\). So the output distributions of them are very different and this would violate privacy protection.

Perhaps, we should propose some fixed parameter \(\beta > 0\) (which could be much smaller than \(\Delta\)), and add a noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\beta}{\varepsilon})\) for whatever \(x \in \mathcal{X}\). For a pair of neighboring \(x, x'\), when \(|q(x) - q(x')| \le \beta\), then the distributions of \(q(x) + N\) and \(q(x') + N\) are indeed \((\varepsilon, 0)\) indistinguishable. The only concern is for the case where \(|q(x) - q(x')| > \beta\) and adding a noise \(N \sim \mathbb{Lap}(\frac{\beta}{\varepsilon})\) fails to protect the privacy.

One simple remedy is simply to refuse to output \(q(x) + N\) (or \(q(x) + N'\)). This should be done in a differentially private manner. Let \[ \{ y \in \mathcal{X} : \delta(y) > \beta \} \]

be the set of points with local sensitivity greater than \(\beta\). For a fixed \(x\), define \(d(x, \{ y : \delta(y) > \beta \})\) be its distance to the set. It follows that for \(x \sim x'\), \[ |d(x, \{ y \in \mathcal{X} : \delta(y) > \beta \}) - d(x', \{ y \in \mathcal{X} : \delta(y) > \beta \})| \le 1. \]

This motivates the following propose-test-release algorithm.

  1. Propose a bound \(\beta\) on local sensitivity.
  2. Compute \(\hat d \doteq d(x, \{ y \in \mathcal{X} : \delta(y) > \beta \}) + \mathbb{Lap}(1 / \varepsilon)\).
  3. If \(\hat d \le \frac{1}{\varepsilon} \ln \frac{1}{\gamma}\) then
    \(\qquad\) output \(\bot\).
  4. Else
    \(\qquad\) output \(q(x) + \mathbb{Lap}(\beta / \varepsilon)\).

Theorem. The algorithm is \((2\varepsilon, \gamma / 2)\) differentially private.

Proof.

First observe that for two neighboring \(x, x'\), their probabilities of outputting \(\bot\) are similar. \[ \Pr[ M(x) = \bot ] \le e^{ \varepsilon} \cdot \Pr[ M(x') = \bot ] \\ \Pr[ M(x) \neq \bot ] \le e^{ \varepsilon} \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \neq \bot ] \]

Now consider a measurable set \(S \subset \mathbb{R}\). \(M(x) \in S\) is only possible when \(\hat d > \frac{1}{\varepsilon} \ln \frac{1}{\gamma}\):
\[ \begin{aligned} \Pr[M(x) \in S] &= \Pr[ M(x) \in S \mid M(x) \neq \bot ] \cdot \Pr[ M(x) \neq \bot] \end{aligned} \]

We consider two cases.

  • Case 1. When \(\delta(x) > \beta\), then \(d(x, \{ y \in \mathcal{X} : \delta(y) > \beta \}) = 0\). It follows that \[ \Pr[M(x) \in S] \le \Pr[M(x) \neq \bot ] \le \frac{\gamma}{2} \]

  • Case 2. When \(\delta(x) \le \beta\), it holds that \(|q(x) - q(x') |\le \beta\) and \[ \Pr[ M(x) \in S \mid M(x) \neq \bot ] \le e^\varepsilon \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \in S \mid M(x') \neq \bot ] \] Therefore, \[ \begin{aligned} \Pr[M(x) \in S] &= \Pr[ M(x) \in S \mid M(x) \neq \bot ] \cdot \Pr[ M(x) \neq \bot] \\ &\le \Pr[ M(x) \in S \mid M(x) \neq \bot ] \cdot e^\varepsilon \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \neq \bot] \\ &\le e^\varepsilon \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \in S \mid M(x') \neq \bot ] \cdot e^\varepsilon \cdot \Pr[ M(x') \neq \bot] \\ &= e^{2\varepsilon} \Pr[ M(x') \in S]. \end{aligned} \]

\(\blacksquare\)

Reference
