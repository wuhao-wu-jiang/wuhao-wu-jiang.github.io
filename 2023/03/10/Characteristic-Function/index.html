Independence

Theorem. Let XXX and YYY be independent random variables.
Then

φ(X+Y)=φ(X)φ(Y). \varphi(X + Y) = \varphi(X) \varphi(Y). φ(X+Y)=φ(X)φ(Y).

Proof.
Since XXX and YYY are independent, the following pairs of random variables are independent

  1. sin⁡(tX(ω))\sin \big( t X(\omega) \big)sin(tX(ω)), sin⁡(tY(ω))\sin \big( t Y(\omega) \big)sin(tY(ω));
  2. cos⁡(tX(ω))\cos \big( t X(\omega) \big)cos(tX(ω)), cos⁡(tY(ω))\cos \big( t Y(\omega) \big)cos(tY(ω));
  3. sin⁡(tX(ω))\sin \big( t X(\omega) \big)sin(tX(ω)), cos⁡(tY(ω))\cos \big( t Y(\omega) \big)cos(tY(ω));
  4. cos⁡(tX(ω))\cos \big( t X(\omega) \big)cos(tX(ω)), sin⁡(tY(ω))\sin \big( t Y(\omega) \big)sin(tY(ω)).

φ(X+Y)=E[eit(X+Y)]=E[(sin⁡(tX(ω))+i⋅cos⁡(tX(ω)))(sin⁡(tY(ω))+i⋅cos⁡(tY(ω)))]=E[sin⁡(tX(ω))]E[sin⁡(tY(ω))]−E[cos⁡(tX(ω))]E[cos⁡(tY(ω))]+iE[sin⁡(tX(ω))]E[cos⁡(tY(ω))]+iE[cos⁡(tX(ω))]E[sin⁡(tY(ω))]=φ(X)φ(Y). \begin{aligned} \varphi(X + Y) &= \mathbb{E} \left[ e^{it(X + Y)} \right] \\ &= \mathbb{E} \left[ \left( \sin \big( t X(\omega) \big) + i \cdot \cos \big( t X(\omega) \big) \right) \left( \sin \big( t Y(\omega) \big) + i \cdot \cos \big( t Y(\omega) \big) \right) \right] \\ &= \mathbb{E} \left[ \sin \big( t X(\omega) \big) \right] \mathbb{E} \left[ \sin \big( t Y(\omega) \big) \right] - \mathbb{E} \left[ \cos \big( t X(\omega) \big) \right] \mathbb{E} \left[ \cos \big( t Y(\omega) \big) \right] \\ &\qquad + i \mathbb{E} \left[ \sin \big( t X(\omega) \big) \right] \mathbb{E} \left[ \cos \big( t Y(\omega) \big) \right] + i \mathbb{E} \left[ \cos \big( t X(\omega) \big) \right] \mathbb{E} \left[ \sin \big( t Y(\omega) \big) \right] \\ &= \varphi(X) \varphi(Y). \end{aligned} φ(X+Y)​=E[eit(X+Y)]=E[(sin(tX(ω))+i⋅cos(tX(ω)))(sin(tY(ω))+i⋅cos(tY(ω)))]=E[sin(tX(ω))]E[sin(tY(ω))]−E[cos(tX(ω))]E[cos(tY(ω))]+iE[sin(tX(ω))]E[cos(tY(ω))]+iE[cos(tX(ω))]E[sin(tY(ω))]=φ(X)φ(Y).​

□\square□
