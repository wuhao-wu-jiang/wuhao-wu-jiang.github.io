It follows that the Fn\mathcal{F}_nFn​ are independent.

□\square□

Expectation

Theorem. If XXX ane YYY are independent and integrable, then

E[XY]=E[X]E[Y]. \mathbb{E}[ X Y ] = \mathbb{E}[ X ] \mathbb{E}[ Y ]. E[XY]=E[X]E[Y].

Proof.
The claim holds trivially if XXX and YYY are simple random variables.

We treat the general case.
There exists simple random variables XnX_nXn​ and YnY_nYn​ such that

  1. Xn→X,∣Xn∣↑∣X∣X_n \rightarrow X, |X_n| \uparrow |X|Xn​→X,∣Xn​∣↑∣X∣ and Yn→Y,∣Yn∣↑∣Y∣Y_n \rightarrow Y, |Y_n| \uparrow |Y|Yn​→Y,∣Yn​∣↑∣Y∣;

  2. The XnX_nXn​ are measurable σ(X)\sigma(X)σ(X) and the YnY_nYn​ are measurable σ(Y)\sigma(Y)σ(Y).

It follows that the XnX_nXn​ and YnY_nYn​ are independent for each n∈N+n \in \mathbb{N}^+n∈N+.
Now E[∣Xn∣⋅∣Yn∣]=E[∣Xn∣]E[∣Yn∣]\mathbb{E}[ |X_n| \cdot |Y_n| ] = \mathbb{E}[ |X_n| ] \mathbb{E}[ |Y_n| ]E[∣Xn​∣⋅∣Yn​∣]=E[∣Xn​∣]E[∣Yn​∣].
Via monotone convergence theorem,

E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣]=lim⁡nE[∣Xn∣⋅∣Yn∣]=lim⁡nE[∣Xn∣]E[∣Yn∣]=E[∣X∣]E[∣Y∣]≤∞. \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}[ |X| \cdot |Y| ] &= \lim_n \mathbb{E}[ |X_n| \cdot |Y_n| ] \\ &= \lim_n \mathbb{E}[ |X_n| ] \mathbb{E}[ |Y_n| ] \\ &= \mathbb{E}[ |X| ] \mathbb{E}[ |Y| ] \\ &\le \infty. \end{aligned} E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣]​=nlim​E[∣Xn​∣⋅∣Yn​∣]=nlim​E[∣Xn​∣]E[∣Yn​∣]=E[∣X∣]E[∣Y∣]≤∞.​

Therefore, E[∣XY∣]=E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣]\mathbb{E}[ |XY| ] = \mathbb{E}[ |X| \cdot |Y| ]E[∣XY∣]=E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣] is integrable.
Via monotone convergence theorem again, we have

lim⁡nE[inf⁡k≥n(2∣XY∣−∣XkYk−XY∣)]=E[lim⁡ninf⁡k≥n(2∣XY∣−∣XkYk−XY∣)]. \begin{aligned} \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ \inf_{k \ge n} \left( 2 |X Y| - |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right)\right] &= \mathbb{E} \left[ \lim_n \inf_{k \ge n} \left( 2 |X Y| - |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right)\right]. \end{aligned} nlim​E[k≥ninf​(2∣XY∣−∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣)]​=E[nlim​k≥ninf​(2∣XY∣−∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣)].​

Therefore,

2E[∣XY∣]−lim⁡nE[sup⁡k≥n∣XkYk−XY∣]=2E[∣XY∣]−E[lim⁡ninf⁡k≥n∣XkYk−XY∣] 2 \mathbb{E}[ |X Y| ] - \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ \sup_{k \ge n} |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right] = 2 \mathbb{E}[ |X Y| ] - \mathbb{E} \left[ \lim_n \inf_{k \ge n} |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right] 2E[∣XY∣]−nlim​E[k≥nsup​∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣]=2E[∣XY∣]−E[nlim​k≥ninf​∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣]

Noting that ∣XY∣|XY|∣XY∣ is integrable and that XkYk→XYX_k Y_k \rightarrow X YXk​Yk​→XY, we have

lim⁡nE[sup⁡k≥n∣XkYk−XY∣]=0, \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ \sup_{k \ge n} |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right] = 0, nlim​E[k≥nsup​∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣]=0,

and

lim⁡n∣E[XnYn]−E[XY]∣≤lim⁡nE[∣XnYn−XY∣]≤lim⁡nE[sup⁡k≥n∣XkYk−XY∣]=0, \begin{aligned} \lim_n |\mathbb{E}[ X_n Y_n] - \mathbb{E}[X Y]| &\le \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ |X_n Y_n - X Y| \right] \\ &\le \lim_n \mathbb{E} \left[ \sup_{k \ge n} |X_k Y_k - X Y| \right] \\ &= 0, \end{aligned} nlim​∣E[Xn​Yn​]−E[XY]∣​≤nlim​E[∣Xn​Yn​−XY∣]≤nlim​E[k≥nsup​∣Xk​Yk​−XY∣]=0,​
