Coupling Lemma

Posted on 2021-11-01 Edited on 2024-07-08

Let μ\muμ and ν\nuν be two probability measures over a finite set Ω\OmegaΩ.

Definition. A probability measure ω\omegaω over Ω×Ω\Omega \times \OmegaΩ×Ω is a coupling of (μ,ν)(\mu, \nu)(μ,ν) if its marginals are μ\muμ and ν\nuν; that is

  1. ∀x∈Ω\forall x \in \Omega∀x∈Ω, ∑y∈Ωω(x,y)=μ(x)\sum_{y \in \Omega} \omega(x, y) = \mu(x)∑y∈Ω​ω(x,y)=μ(x),

  2. ∀y∈Ω\forall y \in \Omega∀y∈Ω, ∑x∈Ωω(x,y)=ν(x)\sum_{x \in \Omega} \omega(x, y) = \nu(x)∑x∈Ω​ω(x,y)=ν(x).

Coupling Lemma

Lemma

  1. For any coupling ω\omegaω of (μ,ν)(\mu, \nu)(μ,ν), if the random variable (X,Y)(X, Y)(X,Y) is distributed according to ω\omegaω, then

Pr⁡[X≠Y]≥∥μ−ν∥TV. \Pr[ X \neq Y ] \ge \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}. Pr[X=Y]≥∥μ−ν∥TV​.

  1. There exists a coupling ω∗\omega^*ω∗ (which is called the maximal coupling) for (μ,ν)(\mu, \nu)(μ,ν) for which

Pr⁡[X≠Y]=∥μ−ν∥TV. \Pr[ X \neq Y ] = \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}. Pr[X=Y]=∥μ−ν∥TV​.

First Proof

Claim 1.
First, observe that for every measurable set S⊆ΩS \subseteq \OmegaS⊆Ω, it holds that

μ(S)−ν(S)=Pr⁡[X∈S]−Pr⁡[Y∈S]≤Pr⁡[X∈S,Y∉S]≤Pr⁡[X≠Y]. \begin{aligned} \mu(S) - \nu(S) = \Pr [ X \in S ] - \Pr [ Y \in S ] \le \Pr[ X \in S, Y \notin S ] \le \Pr[ X \neq Y ]. \end{aligned} μ(S)−ν(S)=Pr[X∈S]−Pr[Y∈S]≤Pr[X∈S,Y∈/S]≤Pr[X=Y].​

It follows that

∥μ−ν∥TV=max⁡ measurable S⊆Ω(μ(S)−ν(S))≤Pr⁡[X≠Y]. \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} = \max_{ \text{ measurable } S \subseteq \Omega} \big( \mu(S) - \nu(S) \big) \le \Pr[ X \neq Y ]. ∥μ−ν∥TV​= measurable S⊆Ωmax​(μ(S)−ν(S))≤Pr[X=Y].

Claim 2.
Next, we show how to construct ω∗\omega^*ω∗.
For each z∈Ωz \in \Omegaz∈Ω, define

ω∗(z,z)=min⁡{μ(z),ν(z)}. \omega^*(z, z) = \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \}. ω∗(z,z)=min{μ(z),ν(z)}.

Clearly it holds that

Pr⁡[X≠Y]=1−∑z∈Ωmin⁡{μ(z),ν(z)}=∥μ−ν∥TV. \begin{aligned} \Pr[ X \neq Y ] = 1 - \sum_{ z \in \Omega } \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \} = \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}. \end{aligned} Pr[X=Y]=1−z∈Ω∑​min{μ(z),ν(z)}=∥μ−ν∥TV​.​

It is left to assign the probability mass ∥μ−ν∥TV\Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}∥μ−ν∥TV​ to (x,y)∈Ω×Ω(x, y) \in \Omega \times \Omega(x,y)∈Ω×Ω, for x≠yx \neq yx=y.

Case 1 (∥μ−ν∥TV=0)\big( \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} = 0 \big)(∥μ−ν∥TV​=0).
Then we can set ω∗(x,y)=0\omega^*(x, y) = 0ω∗(x,y)=0 for all x≠yx \neq yx=y.
Further, it holds that μ(z)=ν(z)\mu(z) = \nu(z)μ(z)=ν(z) for all z∈Ωz \in \Omegaz∈Ω.
Therefore,

∑y∈Ωw∗(x,y)=w∗(x,x)=min⁡{μ(x),ν(x)}=μ(x),∑x∈Ωw∗(x,y)=w∗(y,y)=min⁡{μ(y),ν(y)}=ν(y) . \begin{aligned} \sum_{ y \in \Omega } w^* (x, y) = w^*(x, x) = \min \{ \mu(x), \nu(x) \} = \mu( x ), \\ \sum_{ x \in \Omega } w^* (x, y) = w^*(y, y) = \min \{ \mu(y), \nu(y) \} = \nu( y )\,. \end{aligned} y∈Ω∑​w∗(x,y)=w∗(x,x)=min{μ(x),ν(x)}=μ(x),x∈Ω∑​w∗(x,y)=w∗(y,y)=min{μ(y),ν(y)}=ν(y).​

Case 2 (∥μ−ν∥TV≠0)\big( \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \neq 0 \big)(∥μ−ν∥TV​=0).
Denote S+≐{z∈Ω:μ(z)≥ν(z)}S_+ \doteq \{ z \in \Omega: \mu(z) \ge \nu(z) \}S+​≐{z∈Ω:μ(z)≥ν(z)}.
Define two probability mass functions

λ1(z)≐μ(z)−ν(z)∥μ−ν∥TV⋅1[z∈S+],λ2(z)≐ν(z)−μ(z)∥μ−ν∥TV⋅1[z∉S+]. \lambda_1 (z) \doteq \frac{ \mu(z) - \nu(z) }{ \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} } \cdot \mathbf{1}[z \in S_+],\qquad \lambda_2 (z) \doteq \frac{ \nu(z) - \mu(z) }{ \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} } \cdot \mathbf{1}[z \notin S_+]. λ1​(z)≐∥μ−ν∥TV​μ(z)−ν(z)​⋅1[z∈S+​],λ2​(z)≐∥μ−ν∥TV​ν(z)−μ(z)​⋅1[z∈/S+​].

Then λ1(Ω)=λ1(S+)=λ2(Ω)=λ2(Sˉ+)=1\lambda_1 ( \Omega ) = \lambda_1 (S_+) = \lambda_2 (\Omega) = \lambda_2 ( \bar S_+ ) = 1λ1​(Ω)=λ1​(S+​)=λ2​(Ω)=λ2​(Sˉ+​)=1.

Now, for x,y∈Ω,x≠yx, y \in \Omega, x \neq yx,y∈Ω,x=y, define

ω∗(x,y)=∥μ−ν∥TV⋅λ1(x)⋅λ2(y). \omega^*(x, y) = \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \cdot \lambda_1 (x) \cdot \lambda_2 (y) . ω∗(x,y)=∥μ−ν∥TV​⋅λ1​(x)⋅λ2​(y).

Also note that for all z∈Ωz \in \Omegaz∈Ω, ∥μ−ν∥TV⋅λ1(z)⋅λ2(z)=0\Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \cdot \lambda_1 (z) \cdot \lambda_2 (z) = 0∥μ−ν∥TV​⋅λ1​(z)⋅λ2​(z)=0.
Therefore,

∑y∈Ωw∗(x,y)=w∗(x,x)+∑y∈Ω: y≠xw∗(x,y)=min⁡{μ(x),ν(x)}+∑y∈Ω∥μ−ν∥TV⋅λ1(x)⋅λ2(y)=min⁡{μ(x),ν(x)}+∥μ−ν∥TV⋅λ1(x)⋅λ2(Ω)=min⁡{μ(x),ν(x)}+(μ(x)−ν(x))⋅1[x∈S+]=μ(x). \begin{aligned} \sum_{ y \in \Omega } w^* (x, y) &= w^*(x, x) + \sum_{ y \in \Omega : \, y \neq x } w^* (x, y) \\ &= \min \{ \mu(x), \nu(x) \} + \sum_{ y \in \Omega } \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \cdot \lambda_1 (x) \cdot \lambda_2 (y) \\ &= \min \{ \mu(x), \nu(x) \} + \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} \cdot \lambda_1 (x) \cdot \lambda_2 (\Omega) \\ &= \min \{ \mu(x), \nu(x) \} + \big( \mu(x) - \nu(x) \big) \cdot \mathbf{1}[x \in S_+] \\ &= \mu(x). \end{aligned} y∈Ω∑​w∗(x,y)​=w∗(x,x)+y∈Ω:y=x∑​w∗(x,y)=min{μ(x),ν(x)}+y∈Ω∑​∥μ−ν∥TV​⋅λ1​(x)⋅λ2​(y)=min{μ(x),ν(x)}+∥μ−ν∥TV​⋅λ1​(x)⋅λ2​(Ω)=min{μ(x),ν(x)}+(μ(x)−ν(x))⋅1[x∈S+​]=μ(x).​

Similarly, we can verify that for each y∈Ωy \in \Omegay∈Ω,

∑y∈Ωω∗(x,y)=ν(y). \sum_{y \in \Omega} \omega^*(x, y) = \nu(y). y∈Ω∑​ω∗(x,y)=ν(y).

□\square□

Second Proof

Claim 1. We see that

Pr⁡[X=Y]=∑z∈ΩPr⁡[X=z,Y=z]≤∑z∈Ωmin⁡{μ(z),ν(z)}. \Pr[X = Y] = \sum_{z \in \Omega} \Pr[X = z, Y = z] \le \sum_{z \in \Omega} \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \}. Pr[X=Y]=z∈Ω∑​Pr[X=z,Y=z]≤z∈Ω∑​min{μ(z),ν(z)}.

Hence,

Pr⁡[X≠Y]=1−Pr⁡[X=Y]≥1−∑z∈Ωmin⁡{μ(z),ν(z)}=∑z∈Ω(μ(z)−min⁡{μ(z),ν(z)})=∥μ−ν∥TV. \begin{aligned} \Pr[X \neq Y] &= 1 - \Pr[X = Y] \ge 1 - \sum_{z \in \Omega} \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \} \\ &= \sum_{z \in \Omega} \Big( \mu(z) - \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \} \Big) = \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}. \end{aligned} Pr[X=Y]​=1−Pr[X=Y]≥1−z∈Ω∑​min{μ(z),ν(z)}=z∈Ω∑​(μ(z)−min{μ(z),ν(z)})=∥μ−ν∥TV​.​

Claim 2.
The case is trivial if ∥μ−ν∥TV\Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV}∥μ−ν∥TV​.
Suppose that η=∥μ−ν∥TV>0\eta = \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} > 0η=∥μ−ν∥TV​>0.

Define

λ(z)≐min⁡{μ(z),ν(z)},∀z∈Ω. \lambda(z) \doteq \min \{ \mu(z), \nu(z) \}, \qquad \forall z \in \Omega. λ(z)≐min{μ(z),ν(z)},∀z∈Ω.

Further, define the distributions π0\pi_0π0​ and π1\pi_1π1​ by

π0(x,y)≐11−η⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y],∀x,y∈Ω,π1(x,y)≐μ(x)−λ(x)η⋅ν(y)−λ(y)η∀x,y∈Ω. \begin{aligned} \pi_0 (x, y) &\doteq \frac{1}{1 - \eta} \cdot \lambda(x) \cdot \mathbb{I}{[x = y]}, & \forall x, y \in \Omega, \\ \pi_1 (x, y) &\doteq \frac{\mu(x) - \lambda(x)}{\eta} \cdot \frac{\nu(y) - \lambda(y)}{ \eta } & \forall x, y \in \Omega. \end{aligned} π0​(x,y)π1​(x,y)​≐1−η1​⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y],≐ημ(x)−λ(x)​⋅ην(y)−λ(y)​​∀x,y∈Ω,∀x,y∈Ω.​

Then π=(1−η)π0+ηπ1\pi = (1 - \eta) \pi_0 + \eta \pi_1π=(1−η)π0​+ηπ1​ is a distribution, whose marginals are

∑y∈Ωπ(x,y)=λ(x)+(μ(x)−λ(x))=μ(x),∑x∈Ωπ(x,y)=λ(y)+(ν(y)−λ(y))=ν(y), \begin{aligned} \sum_{y \in \Omega} \pi(x, y) &= \lambda(x) + \big( \mu(x) - \lambda(x) \big) = \mu(x), \\ \sum_{x \in \Omega} \pi(x, y) &= \lambda(y) + \big( \nu(y) - \lambda(y) \big) = \nu(y), \end{aligned} y∈Ω∑​π(x,y)x∈Ω∑​π(x,y)​=λ(x)+(μ(x)−λ(x))=μ(x),=λ(y)+(ν(y)−λ(y))=ν(y),​

where we use the fact that

∥μ−ν∥TV=∑x∈Ω(μ(x)−λ(x))=∑y∈Ω(ν(y)−λ(y)). \Vert \mu - \nu \Vert_{TV} = \sum_{x \in \Omega} \big(\mu(x) - \lambda(x) \big) = \sum_{y \in \Omega} \big( \nu(y) - \lambda(y) \big). ∥μ−ν∥TV​=x∈Ω∑​(μ(x)−λ(x))=y∈Ω∑​(ν(y)−λ(y)).

Finally, consider the marginals of π0\pi_0π0​ and π1\pi_1π1​:

π0(x,⋅)≐∑y∈Ω11−η⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y]=11−η⋅λ(x)∀x∈Ω,π0(⋅,y)≐∑x∈Ω11−η⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y]=11−η⋅λ(y)∀y∈Ω,π1(x,⋅)≐∑y∈Ωμ(x)−λ(x)η⋅ν(y)−λ(y)η=μ(x)−λ(x)η∀x∈Ω,π1(⋅,y)≐∑x∈Ωμ(x)−λ(x)η⋅ν(y)−λ(y)η=ν(y)−λ(y)η∀y∈Ω, \begin{aligned} \pi_0 (x, \cdot) &\doteq \sum_{y \in \Omega} \frac{1}{1 - \eta} \cdot \lambda(x) \cdot \mathbb{I}{[x = y]} = \frac{1}{1 - \eta} \cdot \lambda(x) & \forall x \in \Omega, \\ \pi_0 (\cdot, y) &\doteq \sum_{x \in \Omega} \frac{1}{1 - \eta} \cdot \lambda(x) \cdot \mathbb{I}{[x = y]} = \frac{1}{1 - \eta} \cdot \lambda(y) & \forall y \in \Omega, \\ \pi_1 (x, \cdot) &\doteq \sum_{y \in \Omega} \frac{\mu(x) - \lambda(x)}{\eta} \cdot \frac{\nu(y) - \lambda(y)}{ \eta } = \frac{\mu(x) - \lambda(x)}{\eta} & \forall x \in \Omega, \\ \pi_1 (\cdot, y) &\doteq \sum_{x \in \Omega} \frac{\mu(x) - \lambda(x)}{\eta} \cdot \frac{\nu(y) - \lambda(y)}{ \eta } = \frac{\nu(y) - \lambda(y)}{ \eta } & \forall y \in \Omega, \end{aligned} π0​(x,⋅)π0​(⋅,y)π1​(x,⋅)π1​(⋅,y)​≐y∈Ω∑​1−η1​⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y]=1−η1​⋅λ(x)≐x∈Ω∑​1−η1​⋅λ(x)⋅I[x=y]=1−η1​⋅λ(y)≐y∈Ω∑​ημ(x)−λ(x)​⋅ην(y)−λ(y)​=ημ(x)−λ(x)​≐x∈Ω∑​ημ(x)−λ(x)​⋅ην(y)−λ(y)​=ην(y)−λ(y)​​∀x∈Ω,∀y∈Ω,∀x∈Ω,∀y∈Ω,​

Then π1(x,⋅)\pi_1 (x, \cdot)π1​(x,⋅) and π1(⋅,y)\pi_1 (\cdot, y)π1​(⋅,y) have disjoint support, and η\etaη is the minimum parameter such that the following condition hold:

μ=(1−η)⋅π0(x,⋅)+η⋅π1(x,⋅),ν=(1−η)⋅π0(⋅,y)+η⋅π1(⋅,y). \mu = (1 - \eta) \cdot \pi_0 (x, \cdot) + \eta \cdot \pi_1 (x, \cdot), \\ \nu = (1 - \eta) \cdot \pi_0 (\cdot, y) + \eta \cdot \pi_1 (\cdot, y). μ=(1−η)⋅π0​(x,⋅)+η⋅π1​(x,⋅),ν=(1−η)⋅π0​(⋅,y)+η⋅π1​(⋅,y).

□\square□
