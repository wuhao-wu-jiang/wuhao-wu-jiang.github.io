Lebesgue Integration of Complex Value Function

Posted on 2022-11-13 Edited on 2022-11-19

Let (Ω,F,μ)(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu)(Ω,F,μ) be a measure space.Let f:Ω→Cf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{C}f:Ω→C be a complex valued function.
It can be written as f(ω)≐g(ω)+ih(ω)f(\omega) \doteq g(\omega) + i h(\omega)f(ω)≐g(ω)+ih(ω), where g,hg, hg,h are both real functions on Ω\OmegaΩ.

Definition [Measurability]. A function fff is measurable F\mathcal{F}F if both ggg and hhh are.

Definition [Integral]. fff is integrable if both ggg and hhh are, and its integral is defined by

∫f dμ≐∫g dμ+i∫h dμ. \int f \, d \mu \doteq \int g \, d \mu + i \int h \, d \mu. ∫fdμ≐∫gdμ+i∫hdμ.

Theorem. fff is integrable if and only if ∣f∣|f|∣f∣ is integrable.

Remark: the integral of the former is complex-valued, while the later is real-valued.

Proof.
The claim follows from that

max⁡{∣g∣,∣h∣}≤∣f∣=∣g∣2+∣h∣2≤∣g∣+∣h∣. \max \{ |g|, |h| \} \le |f| = \sqrt{|g|^2 + |h|^2} \le |g| + |h|. max{∣g∣,∣h∣}≤∣f∣=∣g∣2+∣h∣2​≤∣g∣+∣h∣.

□\square□

Theorem [Linearity]. Let f1,f2f_1, f_2f1​,f2​ be integrable.
Then f1+f2f_1 + f_2f1​+f2​ is integrable, and

∫f1+f2 dμ=∫f1 dμ+∫f2 dμ. \int f_1 + f_2 \, d \mu = \int f_1 \, d \mu + \int f_2 \, d \mu. ∫f1​+f2​dμ=∫f1​dμ+∫f2​dμ.

Proof.
The integrability of f1+f2f_1 + f_2f1​+f2​ follows from the one of ∣f1+f2∣|f_1 + f_2|∣f1​+f2​∣, which is integrable as ∣f1+f2∣≤∣f1∣+∣f2∣|f_1 + f_2| \le |f_1| + |f_2|∣f1​+f2​∣≤∣f1​∣+∣f2​∣.

To prove linearity, let f1=g1+ih1f_1 = g_1 + i h_1f1​=g1​+ih1​ and f2=g2+ih2f_2 = g_2 + i h_2f2​=g2​+ih2​.
Then

∫f1+f2 dμ≐∫g1+g2 dμ+i∫h1+h2 dμ=∫g1 dμ+i∫h1 dμ+∫g2 dμ+i∫h2 dμ≐∫f1 dμ+∫f2 dμ. \begin{aligned} \int f_1 + f_2 \, d \mu &\doteq \int g_1 + g_2 \, d \mu + i \int h_1 + h_2 \, d \mu \\ &= \int g_1 \, d \mu + i \int h_1 \, d \mu + \int g_2 \, d \mu + i \int h_2 \, d \mu \\ &\doteq \int f_1 \, d \mu + \int f_2 \, d \mu. \end{aligned} ∫f1​+f2​dμ​≐∫g1​+g2​dμ+i∫h1​+h2​dμ=∫g1​dμ+i∫h1​dμ+∫g2​dμ+i∫h2​dμ≐∫f1​dμ+∫f2​dμ.​

□\square□

Theorem. If fff is integrable, then

∣∫f dμ∣≤∫∣f∣ dμ. \left| \int f \, d \mu \right| \le \int |f| \, d \mu. ∣∣∣∣∣​∫fdμ∣∣∣∣∣​≤∫∣f∣dμ.

Proof. Let f=g+ihf = g + i hf=g+ih.
Consider first that ggg and hhh are simple functions.
W.L.O.G., assume g=∑k∈[n]xkIAkg = \sum_{k \in [n]} x_k \mathbb{I}_{A_k}g=∑k∈[n]​xk​IAk​​, h=∑k∈[n]ykIAkh = \sum_{k \in [n]} y_k \mathbb{I}_{A_k}h=∑k∈[n]​yk​IAk​​, where Ak∈F,k∈[n]A_k \in \mathcal{F}, k \in [n]Ak​∈F,k∈[n] constitute a partition of Ω\OmegaΩ.

∣∫f dμ∣=∣∫g dμ+i∫h dμ∣=∣∑k∈[n]xk⋅μ(Ak)+i∑k∈[n]yk⋅μ(Ak)∣=∣∑k∈[n](xk+iyk)⋅μ(Ak)∣≤∑k∈[n]∣(xk+iyk)⋅μ(Ak)∣=∑k∈[n]∣(xk+iyk)∣⋅μ(Ak)=∫∣f∣ dμ. \begin{aligned} \left| \int f \, d \mu \right| &= \left|\int g \, d \mu + i \int h \, d \mu \right| \\ &= \left| \sum_{k \in [n]} x_k \cdot \mu (A_k) + i \sum_{k \in [n]} y_k \cdot \mu (A_k) \right| \\ &= \left| \sum_{k \in [n]} \big( x_k + i y_k \big) \cdot \mu (A_k) \right| \\ &\le \sum_{k \in [n]} \left| \big( x_k + i y_k \big) \cdot \mu (A_k) \right| \\ &= \sum_{k \in [n]} \left| \big( x_k + i y_k \big) \right| \cdot \mu (A_k) \\ &= \int |f| \, d \mu. \end{aligned} ∣∣∣∣∣​∫fdμ∣∣∣∣∣​​=∣∣∣∣∣​∫gdμ+i∫hdμ∣∣∣∣∣​=∣∣∣∣∣∣∣​k∈[n]∑​xk​⋅μ(Ak​)+ik∈[n]∑​yk​⋅μ(Ak​)∣∣∣∣∣∣∣​=∣∣∣∣∣∣∣​k∈[n]∑​(xk​+iyk​)⋅μ(Ak​)∣∣∣∣∣∣∣​≤k∈[n]∑​∣∣∣​(xk​+iyk​)⋅μ(Ak​)∣∣∣​=k∈[n]∑​∣∣∣​(xk​+iyk​)∣∣∣​⋅μ(Ak​)=∫∣f∣dμ.​

For general integrable f=g+ihf = g + i hf=g+ih, there exists simple functions gn,hng_n, h_ngn​,hn​ such that

  1. lim⁡ngn=g\lim_n g_n = glimn​gn​=g and lim⁡nhn=h\lim_n h_n = hlimn​hn​=h;

  2. ∣gn∣≤∣g∣|g_n| \le |g|∣gn​∣≤∣g∣ and ∣hn∣≤∣h∣|h_n| \le |h|∣hn​∣≤∣h∣ for all n∈N+n \in \mathbb{N}^+n∈N+;

  3. lim⁡n∫gn dμ=∫g dμ\lim_n \int g_n \, d \mu = \int g \, d \mulimn​∫gn​dμ=∫gdμ and lim⁡n∫hn dμ=∫h dμ\lim_n \int h_n \, d \mu = \int h \, d \mulimn​∫hn​dμ=∫hdμ.

It follows that ∣gn+ihn∣↑∣f∣|g_n + i h_n| \uparrow |f|∣gn​+ihn​∣↑∣f∣.
Hence,

∣∫f dμ∣=∣∫g dμ+i∫h dμ∣=∣lim⁡n∫(gn+ihn) dμ∣=lim⁡n∣∫gn+ihn dμ∣≤(a)lim⁡n∫∣gn+ihn∣ dμ=(b)∫∣f∣ dμ, \begin{aligned} \left| \int f \, d \mu \right| &= \left|\int g \, d \mu + i \int h \, d \mu \right| \\ &= \left| \lim_n \int \left( g_n + i h_n \right)\, d \mu \right| \\ &= \lim_n \left| \int g_n + i h_n \, d \mu \right| \\ &\stackrel{(a)}{\le} \lim_n \int \left| g_n + i h_n \right| \, d \mu \\ &\stackrel{(b)}{=} \int |f| \, d \mu, \end{aligned} ∣∣∣∣∣​∫fdμ∣∣∣∣∣​​=∣∣∣∣∣​∫gdμ+i∫hdμ∣∣∣∣∣​=∣∣∣∣∣​nlim​∫(gn​+ihn​)dμ∣∣∣∣∣​=nlim​∣∣∣∣∣​∫gn​+ihn​dμ∣∣∣∣∣​≤(a)​nlim​∫∣gn​+ihn​∣dμ=(b)∫∣f∣dμ,​

Theorem. Suppose that fn=gn+ihnf_n = g_n + i h_nfn​=gn​+ihn​ are complex integrable functions satisfying ∑n≥1∫∣fn∣ dμ<∞\sum_{n \ge 1} \int |f_n| \, d \mu < \infty∑n≥1​∫∣fn​∣dμ<∞.
Then ∑n≥1fn\sum_{n \ge 1} f_n∑n≥1​fn​ is integrable, and

∫∑n≥1fn dμ=∑n≥1∫fn dμ. \int \sum_{n \ge 1} f_n \, d \mu = \sum_{n \ge 1} \int f_n \, d \mu. ∫n≥1∑​fn​dμ=n≥1∑​∫fn​dμ.

Proof.
By monotone convergence theorem, we have

∫∑n≥1∣fn∣ dμ=∑n≥1∫∣fn∣ dμ<∞. \int \sum_{n \ge 1} |f_n| \, d \mu = \sum_{n \ge 1} \int |f_n| \, d \mu < \infty. ∫n≥1∑​∣fn​∣dμ=n≥1∑​∫∣fn​∣dμ<∞.

Since

∣∑n≥1gn∣≤∑n≥1∣gn∣≤∑n≥1∣fn∣, \Bigg| \sum_{n \ge 1} g_n \Bigg| \le \sum_{n \ge 1} |g_n| \le \sum_{n \ge 1} |f_n|, ∣∣∣∣∣∣​n≥1∑​gn​∣∣∣∣∣∣​≤n≥1∑​∣gn​∣≤n≥1∑​∣fn​∣,

∑n≥1gn\sum_{n \ge 1} g_n∑n≥1​gn​ is integrable.
Via dominated convergence theorem,

∫∑n≥1gn dμ=∑n≥1∫gn dμ. \int \sum_{n \ge 1} g_n \, d \mu = \sum_{n \ge 1} \int g_n \, d \mu. ∫n≥1∑​gn​dμ=n≥1∑​∫gn​dμ.

Similar argument shows that ∑n≥1hn\sum_{n \ge 1} h_n∑n≥1​hn​ is integrable, and

∫∑n≥1hn dμ=∑n≥1∫hn dμ. \int \sum_{n \ge 1} h_n \, d \mu = \sum_{n \ge 1} \int h_n \, d \mu. ∫n≥1∑​hn​dμ=n≥1∑​∫hn​dμ.

Therefore, ∑n≥1fn\sum_{n \ge 1} f_n∑n≥1​fn​ is integrable, and

∫∑n≥1fn dμ≐∫∑n≥1gn dμ+i∫∑n≥1hn dμ=∑n≥1∫gn dμ+∑n≥1i∫hn dμ≐∑n≥1∫fn dμ. \begin{aligned} \int \sum_{n \ge 1} f_n \, d \mu &\doteq \int \sum_{n \ge 1} g_n \, d \mu + i \int \sum_{n \ge 1} h_n \, d \mu \\ &= \sum_{n \ge 1} \int g_n \, d \mu + \sum_{n \ge 1} i \int h_n \, d \mu \\ &\doteq \sum_{n \ge 1} \int f_n \, d \mu. \end{aligned} ∫n≥1∑​fn​dμ​≐∫n≥1∑​gn​dμ+i∫n≥1∑​hn​dμ=n≥1∑​∫gn​dμ+n≥1∑​i∫hn​dμ≐n≥1∑​∫fn​dμ.​

□\square□
