Fubini’s Theorem

Integration is a generalization of discrete sum.
Given a measure space $(\Omega, \sigma( \mathcal{S} ), \bar \mu)$, and a measure function $f$, the integral of $f$ exists, if

  1. $f \ge 0$;
  2. Or $\int |f| d\, \bar \mu < \infty$.

In case that $(\Omega, \sigma( \mathcal{S} ), \bar \mu)$ is a produce measure space, the Fubini’s theorem states that if the integral exists, it can be computed by iterated integrals.

Theorem (Fubini’s Theorem). If $f \ge 0$ or $\int |f| d\, \bar \mu < \infty$, then there exists $A_i \in \mathcal{F}_i, i \in [k]$, s.t., $\mu_i (A_i) = 0$, and for each permutation $\pi$ of $[k]$,

Remark. For the case concerning $f \ge 0$, we have $A_i = \emptyset, i \in [k]$, and the Theorem is called Tonelli’s theorem.
