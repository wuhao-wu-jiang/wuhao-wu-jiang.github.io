Entropy

We rely on sub-additivity of entropy.

Let XXX and YYY be discrete random variables, then

  1. H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y)H( X, Y ) \le H(X) + H(Y)H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y).
  2. H(X∣Y)≤H(X)H(X | Y) \le H(X)H(X∣Y)≤H(X).

Proof.

H(X)=log⁡Vol(r). H(X) = \log \mathbb{Vol}(r). H(X)=logVol(r).

H(X)≤H(X1)+H(X2)+...+H(Xn)=nH(Y∣I)≤nH(Y). \begin{aligned} H(X) &\le H(X_1) + H(X_2) + ... + H(X_n) \\ &= n H(Y \mid I) \\ &\le n H(Y). \end{aligned} H(X)​≤H(X1​)+H(X2​)+...+H(Xn​)=nH(Y∣I)≤nH(Y).​
