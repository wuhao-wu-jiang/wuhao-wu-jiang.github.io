Introduction

Properties

Polynomials have two fundamental properties:

  1. A non-zero polynomial of degree ddd has at most ddd distinct roots.

Key-Sharing

Suppose that we have nnn-candidates who knows some information about the key and we require that only when no less than kkk of them aggragate their information can they figure the actual key. No group of less than kkk candidates can achieve this. The way we do it is as follows:

  1. Select a large prime number ppp.
  2. Select k+1k+1k+1 random numbers y0,y1,...,yky_0, y_1, ..., y_ky0​,y1​,...,yk​ from [p−1][p-1][p−1].
  3. Generate the unique polynomial p(x)p(x)p(x) with degree kkk that goes through (0,y0),(1,y1),...,(k,yk)(0, y_0), (1, y_1), ..., (k, y_k)(0,y0​),(1,y1​),...,(k,yk​).
  4. Inform the value of p(1)p(1)p(1) to the first person, p(2)p(2)p(2) to the second one, … and p(n)p(n)p(n) to the nnn-th person.

If k−1k - 1k−1 people pool their keys together, then there are ppp possible polynomials that goes through all their keys, each has different value at point 0. The probability of guessing the correct key is given by 1p\frac{1}{p}p1​.
