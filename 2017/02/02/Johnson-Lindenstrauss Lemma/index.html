Johnson-Lindenstrauss Lemma

Posted on 2017-02-02 Edited on 2021-03-13

Last modified date 19-Jan-2021.

Problem

Let U⊂Rd\mathcal{U} \subset \mathbb{R}^dU⊂Rd be a set of nnn points. We want to find a linear transformation A∈Rk×dA \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times d}A∈Rk×d that embeds the points in U\mathcal{U}U to Rk\mathbb{R}^kRk while preserving the pair-wise Euclidean distances.

If k≥dk \ge dk≥d, the problem is trivial. There is an isomorphic mapping between Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd and a ddd-dimensional subspace in Rk\mathbb{R}^kRk. Let u1,...,ud∈Rku_1, ..., u_d \in \mathbb{R}^ku1​,...,ud​∈Rk and e1,...,ed∈Rde_1, ..., e_d \in \mathbb{R}^de1​,...,ed​∈Rd be unit vectors, such that for i∈[d]i \in [d]i∈[d], uiu_iui​ (eie_iei​) has the iii-th entry equal to 1 and other entries equal to 0. Then one possible isomorphic mapping is

A=u1e1T+...+udedT.A = u_1 e_1^T + ... + u_d e_d^T. A=u1​e1T​+...+ud​edT​.

If k<dk < dk<d, then such an isomorphic embedding is impossible. However, it is feasible if we aim to preserve the pair-wise distance up to a multiplicative error ϵ∈(0,1)\epsilon \in (0, 1)ϵ∈(0,1) with high probability: ∀x,y∈U\forall x, y \in \mathcal{U}∀x,y∈U,

∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2∈(1±ϵ)∣∣x−y∣∣2.||A x - A y ||^2 \in (1 \pm \epsilon) || x - y ||^2. ∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2∈(1±ϵ)∣∣x−y∣∣2.

The smaller kkk is, the less space we need to store the embedded vectors AU={Ax:x∈U}A \mathcal{U} = \{ Ax : x \in \mathcal{U} \}AU={Ax:x∈U}. It remains to study how small kkk can be?

Theorem. For any set U⊂Rd\mathcal{U} \subset \mathbb{R}^dU⊂Rd of nnn-points, there is a matrix A∈Rk×dA \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times d}A∈Rk×d, such that with probability at least 1−1n1 - \frac{1}{n}1−n1​,

∀x,y∈U,∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2∈(1±ϵ)∣∣x−y∣∣2, \forall x, y \in \mathcal{U}, ||A x - A y ||^2 \in (1 \pm \epsilon) || x - y ||^2, ∀x,y∈U,∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2∈(1±ϵ)∣∣x−y∣∣2,

where k=O(log⁡nϵ2)k = O\left( \frac{ \log n }{ \epsilon^2} \right)k=O(ϵ2logn​).

The value of kkk depends only on nnn but not ddd.

Properties

Column Perspective

On the high level, we try to squeeze ddd (in expectation) orthonormal vectors into a smaller space Rk\mathbb{R}^kRk. We do this by generating a set of ddd random vectors u1,u2,...,ud∈Rku_1, u_2, ..., u_d \in \mathbb{R}^ku1​,u2​,...,ud​∈Rk, such that:

  1. Each coordinate of uiu_iui​ is generated independently for i∈[d]i \in [d]i∈[d];

and in expectation, they are orthonormal:

  1. E[∣∣ui∣∣2]=1\mathbb{E} \big[ ||u_i ||^2 \big] = 1E[∣∣ui​∣∣2]=1 for i∈[d]i \in [d]i∈[d];
  2. E[<ui,uj>]=0\mathbb{E} \big[ \left< u_i, u_j \right> \big] = 0E[⟨ui​,uj​⟩]=0 for i,j∈[d],i≠ji, j \in [d], i \neq ji,j∈[d],i​=j.

Let e1,...,ed∈Rde_1, ..., e_d \in \mathbb{R}^de1​,...,ed​∈Rd be unit vectors in Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd and eie_iei​ (i∈[d]i \in [d]i∈[d]) has the iii-th entry equal to 1. Define

A=[u1,u2,...,ud]=u1e1T+...+udedT.A = \begin{bmatrix} u_1, u_2, ..., u_d \end{bmatrix} = u_1 e_1^T + ... + u_d e_d^T. A=[u1​,u2​,...,ud​​]=u1​e1T​+...+ud​edT​.

For a fixed pair x,y∈Ux, y \in \mathcal{U}x,y∈U, define z=x−yz = x - yz=x−y. Then

∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2=∣∣Az∣∣2=∣∣∑i∈[d]z[i]⋅ui∣∣2=∑i∈[d](z[i])2∣∣ui∣∣2+∑i,j∈[d],i≠j(z[i]⋅z[j])⋅<ui,uj>. \begin{aligned} || A x - A y ||^2 &= || A z ||^2 \\ &= || \sum_{ i \in [d] } z[i] \cdot u_i||^2 \\ &= \sum_{i \in [d] } (z[i])^2 ||u_i||^2 + \sum_{ i, j \in [d], i \neq j} (z[i] \cdot z[j]) \cdot \left< u_i, u_j \right>. \end{aligned} ∣∣Ax−Ay∣∣2​=∣∣Az∣∣2=∣∣i∈[d]∑​z[i]⋅ui​∣∣2=i∈[d]∑​(z[i])2∣∣ui​∣∣2+i,j∈[d],i​=j∑​(z[i]⋅z[j])⋅⟨ui​,uj​⟩.​

Taking expectation of both side, and by assumption of vt,t∈[d]v_t, t \in [d]vt​,t∈[d], it holds that

E[∣∣z∣∣2]=∣∣z∣∣2.\mathbb{ E } \big[ ||z||^2 \big] = ||z||^2. E[∣∣z∣∣2]=∣∣z∣∣2.
