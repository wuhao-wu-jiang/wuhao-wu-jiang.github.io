The key result of this section is the following theorem.

Theorem. An nnn-dimension discrete Fourier transform can be performed in O(nlog⁡n)O(n \log n)O(nlogn) time. Equivalently, given v∈Cnv \in \mathbb{C}^nv∈Cn, MnvM_n vMn​v can be computed in O(nlog⁡n)O(n \log n)O(nlogn) time.

The underlying idea of the theorem is divide-and-conquer. Let T(n)T(n)T(n) be the time of performing an nnn-dimension discrete Fourier transform. We will prove the following recursion

T(n)=2⋅T(n/2)+O(n)T(n) = 2 \cdot T(n / 2) + O(n)T(n)=2⋅T(n/2)+O(n).

And the Fast Fourier Transform algorithm is shown as below.

Algorithm FFT (Fast Fourier Transform).
Input. v∈Cnv \in \mathbb{C}^nv∈Cn.

  1. If n=1n = 1n=1, return vvv.
  2. Compute re←Mn/2ver_e \leftarrow M_{n / 2} v_ere​←Mn/2​ve​ by FFT(vev_eve​).
  3. Compute ro←Mn/2vor_o \leftarrow M_{n / 2} v_oro​←Mn/2​vo​ by FFT(vov_ovo​).
  4. Return

r←[re+diag(e−2⋅0⋅πin,e−2⋅1⋅πin,e−2⋅2⋅πin,e−2⋅3⋅πin)rore−diag(e−2⋅0⋅πin,e−2⋅1⋅πin,e−2⋅2⋅πin,e−2⋅3⋅πin)ro]. r \leftarrow \begin{bmatrix} r_e + \text{diag} \left( e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 0 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 1 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 2 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 3 \cdot \pi i}{ n} } \right) r_o \\ r_e - \text{diag} \left( e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 0 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 1 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 2 \cdot \pi i}{ n} }, e^{ -\frac{2 \cdot 3 \cdot \pi i}{ n} } \right) r_o \end{bmatrix}. r←⎣⎡​re​+diag(e−n2⋅0⋅πi​,e−n2⋅1⋅πi​,e−n2⋅2⋅πi​,e−n2⋅3⋅πi​)ro​re​−diag(e−n2⋅0⋅πi​,e−n2⋅1⋅πi​,e−n2⋅2⋅πi​,e−n2⋅3⋅πi​)ro​​⎦⎤​.

Both step 2 and 3 have time T(n/2)T(n / 2)T(n/2). Step 4 aggregates the results and has time O(n)O(n)O(n), which implies that
