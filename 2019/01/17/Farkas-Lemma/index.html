Farkas' Lemma

Posted on 2019-01-17 Edited on 2020-01-31

Given A∈Rm×nA \in R^{m \times n}A∈Rm×n and b∈Rnb \in R^nb∈Rn, the Farkas Lemma states that exactly one of the following holds:

  1. ∃x∈Rn​:Ax=b\exists x \in R^n​ : Ax = b∃x∈Rn​:Ax=b, x≥0x \ge 0x≥0,
  2. ∃y∈Rm:yTA≤0\exists y \in R^m : y^T A \le 0∃y∈Rm:yTA≤0, yTb>0y^T b > 0yTb>0.

Geometrically, the first statements says that bbb is a non-negative linear combination of columns of AAA. Denote the conic combination of columns of AAA as P={Ax:∀x≥0}⊂RmP = \{ Ax: \forall x \ge 0\} \subset R^mP={Ax:∀x≥0}⊂Rm. Note that it is a subset of the columns space of AAA.

If b∉Pb \notin Pb∈/P, the second statement is equivalent to that

  1. ∃y∈Rm\exists y \in R^m∃y∈Rm, such that yTb>0y^T b > 0yTb>0 and ∀z∈P\forall z \in P∀z∈P, we have yTz≤0y^T z \le 0yTz≤0.

When (3) holds, clearly (2) holds, as columns of AAA belong to PPP. On the other hand, every element in PPP is conic combination of columns of AAA, then ∀z=Ax∈P\forall z = Ax \in P∀z=Ax∈P, then yTz=yT(Ax)=(yTA)x≤0y^T z = y^T (A x) = (y^T A) x \le 0yTz=yT(Ax)=(yTA)x≤0 by the fact that yTA≤0∧x≥0y^T A \le 0 \wedge x \ge 0yTA≤0∧x≥0.
