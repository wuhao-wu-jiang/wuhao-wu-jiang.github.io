Total Law of Variance

Posted on 2020-05-23 Edited on 2021-02-07

Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be random variables. Then the conditional expectation \[ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \] is a random variable that depends on \(X\). Correspondingly we define its variance as \[ \mathbb{Var} [ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X]] = \mathbb{E} \left[ \left( \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right)^2 \right] - ( \mathbb{E} \left[ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right] )^2 \] The Law of Total Variance states that \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{Var} [Y] &= \mathbb{E} [Y^2] - \left( \mathbb{E} [Y] \right)^2 \\ &= \mathbb{E} [ \mathbb{E} [Y^2 \mid X]] - \mathbb{E} \left[ \left( \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right)^2 \right] + \mathbb{E} \left[ \left( \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right)^2 \right] - \left( \mathbb{E} \left[ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right] \right)^2 \\ &= \mathbb{E} \left[ \mathbb{E} [Y^2 \mid X] - \left( \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] \right)^2 \right] + \mathbb{Var} [ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] ] \\ &= \mathbb{E} \left[ \mathbb{Var} [Y \mid X] \right] + \mathbb{Var} [ \mathbb{E} [Y \mid X] ] \end{aligned} \] \(\blacksquare\)
