Chinese-Reminder-Theorem

Applications

The theorem has many interesting applications. Here are some:

Theorem 1. Let φ(n)\varphi(n)φ(n) be the numbers in [n][n][n] that are coprime with nnn.
If n=pqn = pqn=pq for primes ppp and qqq, then φ(n)=(p−1)(q−1)\varphi(n) = (p - 1)(q - 1)φ(n)=(p−1)(q−1).

Proof. By Chinese Reminder Theorem, there is a one-to-one correspondence between [n][n][n] and [p]×[q][p] \times [q][p]×[q].
If x∈[n]x \in [n]x∈[n] is not coprime with nnn, either p∣xp \mid xp∣x or q∣xq \mid xq∣x.

The number of such pairs is given by p+q−1p + q - 1p+q−1.

□\square□
