By law of large number, μ~\tilde \muμ~​ converges to μ\muμ when we sample enough numbers of XXX. To search for the interval [12j,12j−1][\frac{1}{2^{j} }, \frac{1}{2^{j - 1} }][2j1​,2j−11​] that μ\muμ belongs to, we perform the test for increasing values of i=1,2,...i = 1, 2, ...i=1,2,... until we find the first iii, such that the test for μ≥12i\mu \ge \frac{1}{2^i}μ≥2i1​ returns true. Finally, after we find an i∈{j−1,j,j+1}i \in \{j - 1, j, j + 1 \}i∈{j−1,j,j+1}, we know that μ≥12⋅2i\mu \ge \frac{1}{2 \cdot 2^i}μ≥2⋅2i1​. Therefore, O(2ilog⁡log⁡npfail)O(2^i \log \frac{\log n}{p_{fail} })O(2ilogpfail​logn​) samples gives an (δ,pfail)(\delta, p_{fail})(δ,pfail​)-estimate of μ\muμ.
