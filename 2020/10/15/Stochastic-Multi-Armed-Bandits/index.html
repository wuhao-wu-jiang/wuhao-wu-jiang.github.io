Stochastic Multi-Armed Bandits

Posted on 2020-10-15 Edited on 2020-10-21

The multi-arm bandit problem (MAB) models a casino with kkk machines, where the reward of each machine is controlled by a fixed and unknown distribution. The player (agent) observes independent random rewards from generated from the distribution of a machine each time it pulls its arm. Because of limited budget, the player can only play the machines TTT times. The goal of the player is to maximize its expected gain.

There is an inherent exploration-exploitation nature of the problem. The player has to explore what is a good machine while simultaneously exploits what seems to be a good machine.

Formulation

We study the restricted case where the reward distribution of each machine is bounded on [0,1][0, 1][0,1]. Define

  1. Di:D_i:Di​: the reward distribution on [0,1][0, 1][0,1] of the iii-th machine, where i∈[k]i \in [k]i∈[k],
  2. μi:\mu_i:μi​: the expected reward of the iii-th machine, where i∈[k]i \in [k]i∈[k],
  3. μ∗=max⁡i∈[k]μi\mu^* = \max_{i \in [k]} \mu_iμ∗=maxi∈[k]​μi​,
  4. i∗:i^*:i∗: the index of the machine with highest expected reward,
  5. Δi=μ∗−μi\Delta_i = \mu^* - \mu_iΔi​=μ∗−μi​.

Let Y⃗i\vec Y_iYi​ be the random reward vector whose elements are i.i.d. random variables from DiD_iDi​:

  1. Y⃗i=(Yi,1,Yi,2,...,Yi,T)\vec Y_i = (Y_{i, 1}, Y_{i, 2}, ..., Y_{i, T})Yi​=(Yi,1​,Yi,2​,...,Yi,T​)

Denote YYY be the matrix of the random variables

Each time the player pulls the arm, it observes one random variable from the matrix. Specially, let

  1. It:I_t:It​: the machine played at the ttt-th trial,
  2. Ni(t):N_i(t):Ni​(t): the number of times the machine iii is played up to trial ttt.

Before trial ttt, the number of random variables observed in Y⃗It\vec Y_{I_t}YIt​​ is NIt(t−1)N_{I_t} (t - 1)NIt​​(t−1). At trial ttt, a new random variable in Y⃗It\vec Y_{I_t}YIt​​ is observed, and NIt(t)=NIt(t−1)+1N_{I_t} (t) = N_{I_t} (t - 1) + 1NIt​​(t)=NIt​​(t−1)+1.   1. Xt≐YIt,NIt(t):X_{t} \doteq Y_{I_t, N_{I_t} (t)}:Xt​≐YIt​,NIt​​(t)​: the rewards obtained at the ttt-th trial.

When the player chooses a machine ItI_tIt​ on trial ttt, it is based on the history of his play, which consists of his past decisions, and the corresponding observed rewards:

  1. Ht:{(I1,X1),(I2,X2),...,(It−1,Xt−1)}H_t: \{ (I_1, X_1), (I_2, X_2), ..., (I_{t -1}, X_{t - 1}) \}Ht​:{(I1​,X1​),(I2​,X2​),...,(It−1​,Xt−1​)} the history up to trial ttt.

The strategy the player uses at trial ttt is considered as a functions from HtH_tHt​ to [k][k][k]:

  1. ϕt:Ht→[k]\phi_t: H_t \rightarrow [k]ϕt​:Ht​→[k] a function that chooses a machine based on the history at trial ttt

We define the policy as the strategies the player uses over all trials:

  1. ϕ=(ϕ1,ϕ2,...,ϕT):\phi = (\phi_1, \phi_2, ..., \phi_T):ϕ=(ϕ1​,ϕ2​,...,ϕT​): policy, the strategy for the pulling arms by the player

The goal is to find a policy that maximizes the expected rewards, or equivalently, the one that minimizes the expected regret
