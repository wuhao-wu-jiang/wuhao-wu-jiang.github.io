Online Matrix Sketch

Posted on 2020-01-01 Edited on 2021-03-12

Background

Given a matrix A∈Rm×nA \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}A∈Rm×n, the goal of low approximation is to find a matrix B∈Rk×nB \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n}B∈Rk×n with rank at most kkk that approximates AAA as well as possible. This can be measured by 222-norm, i.e.,

∣∣A−B∣∣=max⁡x∈Rn,∣∣x∣∣=1∣∣(A−B)x∣∣||A - B|| = \max_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n, ||x|| = 1} ||(A - B)x|| ∣∣A−B∣∣=x∈Rn,∣∣x∣∣=1max​∣∣(A−B)x∣∣

If we know the entire matrix AAA, its best approximation is given by the singular vector decomposition,

B≐∑i=1kσiuiviT B \doteq \sum_{i = 1}^k \sigma_i u_i v_i^T B≐i=1∑k​σi​ui​viT​

where σi\sigma_iσi​, uiu_iui​ and viv_ivi​ are the iii-th largest singular value, left singular vector and right singular vector of AAA, respectively.

When some elements of AAA are missing, finding such a matrix BBB to approximate AAA as well as to predict the missing entries of AAA is known as collaborative filtering, which is used in recommendation system.

Problem

One scenario is to approximate AAA in an online fashion, i.e., the rows of AAA comes on by one (no missing values). Denote the iii-th row of AAA as AiA_iAi​. We need to maintain a smaller matrix B∈Rk×nB \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times n}B∈Rk×n, termed the matrix sketch of AAA, that approximate AAA well, in the sense that

∣∣A−B∣∣≤2∣∣A∣∣F2k,|| A - B || \le \frac{2 ||A||_F^2}{ k }, ∣∣A−B∣∣≤k2∣∣A∣∣F2​​,

where ∣∣A∣∣F≐∑i∈[n],j∈[n]Ai,j2|| A ||_F \doteq \sqrt{ \sum_{i \in [n], j \in [n] } A_{i,j}^2 }∣∣A∣∣F​≐∑i∈[n],j∈[n]​Ai,j2​​ is the Frobenius norm of AAA.

Algorithm

Algorithm [1]

  1. B←B \leftarrowB← k×n{k \times n}k×n all zero matrix.
  2. For i∈[1:m]i \in [1:m]i∈[1:m]:
  3. \qquad Insert AiA_iAi​ into a zero row of BBB.
  4. \qquad If BBB has no zero row after insertion:
  5. \qquad \qquad Ui,Σi−,Vi←SVD(B)U_i, \Sigma_{i-}, V_i \leftarrow \text{SVD} (B)Ui​,Σi−​,Vi​←SVD(B), such that B=UiΣi−ViTB = U_i \Sigma_{i-} V_i^TB=Ui​Σi−​ViT​.
  6. \qquad\qquad Σi←max⁡{Σi−2−σi,k/22I,0}\Sigma_{i} \leftarrow \sqrt{ \max \{ \Sigma_{i-}^2 - \sigma_{i, k / 2}^2 I, 0 \} }Σi​←max{Σi−2​−σi,k/22​I,0}​
  7. \qquad\qquad B←ΣiViTB \leftarrow \Sigma_{i} V_i^TB←Σi​ViT​.

Here σi,k/2\sigma_{i, k / 2}σi,k/2​ is the k/2k / 2k/2-th largest singular value of Σi−\Sigma_{i -}Σi−​. The line 6 throws away all singular values that are less than σi,k/2\sigma_{i, k / 2}σi,k/2​, and then subtract the squares of all singular values that are greater by σi,k/22\sigma_{i, k / 2}^2σi,k/22​.
