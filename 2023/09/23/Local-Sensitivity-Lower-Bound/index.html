Proof.
Let

\begin{align*} G_b &= \left\{ y \in \mathbb{R} : |y − q(x_b)| < |q(x_0) − q(x_1)| \right\}, \quad \forall b \in \{0, 1\}, \\ p &= \min \{ \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ], \Pr[ M(x_1) \in G_1 ] \}. \end{align*}

We present two ways of deriving it.

First,

\begin{align*} 2 \cdot p &\le \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ] + \Pr[ M(x_1) \in G_1 ] \\ &\le \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ] + e^\varepsilon \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_1 ] + \delta \\ &\le \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ] + e^\varepsilon \big(1 - \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ]\big) + \delta \\ &= e^\varepsilon + \delta + (1 - e^\varepsilon) \cdot \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_0 ]. \end{align*}

Similarly, we have 2⋅p≤eε+δ+(1−eε)⋅Pr⁡[M(x1)∈G1]2 \cdot p \le e^\varepsilon + \delta + (1 - e^\varepsilon) \cdot \Pr[ M(x_1) \in G_1 ]2⋅p≤eε+δ+(1−eε)⋅Pr[M(x1​)∈G1​].

Second Proof:

\begin{align*} 1 − p &\ge \Pr[ M(x_0) \notin G_0 ] \\ &\ge \Pr[ M(x_0) \in G_1] \\ &\ge e^{− \varepsilon} \cdot \Pr[ M(x_1) \in G_1 ] − \delta \\ &\ge e^{− \varepsilon} \cdot p − \delta. \end{align*}
