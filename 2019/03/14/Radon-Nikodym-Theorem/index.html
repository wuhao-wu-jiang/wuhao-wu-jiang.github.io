Radon Nikodym Theorem

Posted on 2019-03-14 Edited on 2022-09-29

We consider a measure space (Ω,F,μ)(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu)(Ω,F,μ), where Ω\OmegaΩ is a set of elements, F\mathcal{F}F is a σ\sigmaσ-field, and μ:F→R\mu : \mathcal{F} \rightarrow Rμ:F→R a σ\sigmaσ-finite measure defined on F\mathcal{F}F.

Definition (Mutually Singular). Measures μ\muμ and ν\nuν on a measurable space (Ω,F)(\Omega, \mathcal{F})(Ω,F) are by definition mutually singular if they have disjoint supports, i.e., if

∃Sμ,Sν∈F,  s.t.  {Sμ∩Sν=∅, μ(Sˉμ)=ν(Sˉν)=0. \exists S_\mu, S_\nu \in \mathcal{F},\, \text{ s.t. }\, \begin{cases} S_\mu \cap S_\nu = \emptyset,\, \\ \mu(\bar S_\mu) = \nu(\bar S_\nu) = 0. \end{cases} ∃Sμ​,Sν​∈F, s.t. {Sμ​∩Sν​=∅,μ(Sˉμ​)=ν(Sˉν​)=0.​

Definition (Absolute Continuous). Measure ν\nuν is absolute continuous with μ\muμ (or dominated by μ\muμ), denoted as ν≪μ\nu \ll \muν≪μ, if

μ(E)=0  ⟹  ν(E)=0,∀E∈F. \mu(E) = 0 \implies \nu(E) = 0, \qquad \forall E \in \mathcal{F}. μ(E)=0⟹ν(E)=0,∀E∈F.

Motivation. Let fff be a nonnegative and measurable function.
Then the measure defined by

ν(E)=∫Ef dμ,∀E∈F, \nu(E) = \int_E f \, d \mu, \qquad \forall E \in \mathcal{F}, ν(E)=∫E​fdμ,∀E∈F,

is absolutely continuous with respect to μ\muμ.
The The Radon-Nikodym Theorem establishes the opposite direction.

The Radon-Nikodym Theorem.
Let μ,ν\mu, \nuμ,ν be σ\sigmaσ-finite measures such that ν≪μ\nu \ll \muν≪μ.
There exists a nonnegative measurable function fff, a density, such that

ν(E)=∫Ef dμ,∀E∈F. \nu(E) = \int_E f \, d \mu, \qquad \forall E \in \mathcal{F}. ν(E)=∫E​fdμ,∀E∈F.

For two such function fff and f′f'f′, μ[f≠f′]=0\mu[ f \neq f' ] = 0μ[f=f′]=0.
fff is called the Radon-Nikodym derivative of ν\nuν with respect to μ\muμ and is denoted dμ/dνd \mu / d \nudμ/dν.

The proof of the Theorem relies on the following lemma, the proof of which is deferred to the end of the article.

Lemma. Let μ\muμ and ν\nuν be two finite measures that are not mutually singular.
Then there exits A∈FA \in \mathcal{F}A∈F and ϵ>0\epsilon > 0ϵ>0, such that μ(A)>0\mu(A) > 0μ(A)>0, and for each measurable set EEE in AAA, it holds that ϵ⋅μ(E)≤ν(E)\epsilon \cdot \mu(E) \le \nu(E)ϵ⋅μ(E)≤ν(E).

μn(E)=μ(E∩En),νn(E)=ν(E∩En),∀E∈F. \mu_n (E) = \mu(E \cap E_n),\quad \nu_n (E) = \nu(E \cap E_n),\quad \forall E \in \mathcal{F}. μn​(E)=μ(E∩En​),νn​(E)=ν(E∩En​),∀E∈F.

Suppose that we can find densities fn,n∈N+f_n, n \in \mathbb{N}^+fn​,n∈N+, such that νn(E)=∫Efn dμn\nu_n(E) = \int_E f_n \, d \mu_nνn​(E)=∫E​fn​dμn​.

Via monotone convergence theorem,

Define the collection of functions
