Least Square Estimation Error Bound

Posted on 2021-06-09

Theorem. Let \(S \subseteq \mathbb{R}^d\) be a symmetric convex body, and let \(\vec{x} \in S\) and \(\vec y = \vec x + \vec a\) for some \(\vec a \in \mathbb{R}^d\). Set \(\vec z = \arg \min_{\vec v \in S} \Vert \vec v - \vec y \Vert_2\). Let \(\vec c = \vec z - \vec x\). Then we must have \[ \Vert \vec c \Vert_2^2 \le 4 \min \Big\{ \Vert \vec a \Vert_2^2, \Vert \vec a \Vert_{S^*} \Big\}, \] where \(\Vert \vec z \Vert_{S^*} \doteq \sup_{ \vec v \in S} | \left< \vec v, \vec a \right>|\).

Proof. Let \(\vec b = \vec z - \vec y\). Then \[ \vec c = \vec a + \vec b. \]

  1. By the definition of \(\vec z\), it holds that \[ \Vert \vec b \Vert_2 \le \Vert \vec a \Vert_2. \] Hence, by triangle inequality, \[ \Vert \vec c \Vert_2 \le \Vert \vec b \Vert_2 + \Vert \vec a \Vert_2 \le 2 \Vert \vec a \Vert_2. \]

  2. In the plot, we notices that \(\left< \vec c, \vec b \right> \le 0 \le \left< \vec c, \vec a \right>\). Formally, \[ \begin{aligned} \left< \vec c, \vec b \right> &= \left< \vec b, \vec b \right> + \left< \vec a, \vec b \right> \\ &= \left< \vec a, \vec b \right> + \Vert \vec b \Vert_2^2 \\ &\le \left< \vec a, \vec b \right> + \Vert \vec a \Vert_2^2 \\ &= \left< \vec c, \vec a \right> \end{aligned} \]

    Hence, \[ \begin{aligned} \Vert \vec c \Vert_2^2 &= \left< \vec c, \vec c \right> \\ &= \left< \vec c, \vec a \right> + \left< \vec c, \vec b \right> \\ &\le \left< \vec c, \vec a \right> + \left< \vec c, \vec a \right>\\ &= 2 \left< \vec c, \vec a \right>\\ &\le 2 \Vert \vec c \Vert_{S} \Vert \vec a \Vert_{S^*} \end{aligned} \]

    where \(\Vert \vec c \Vert_S \doteq \inf \{ r \in \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0} : \vec c \in r S \}\) and the last inequality follows from Holder Inequality. As \[ \Vert \vec c \Vert_{S} = \Vert \vec z - \vec x \Vert_{S} \le \Vert \vec z \Vert_{S} + \Vert \vec x \Vert_{S} \le 2, \] we get \(\Vert \vec c \Vert_{S} \le 4 \Vert \vec a \Vert_{S^*}\).

Reference

[1] Nikolov, Aleksandar, Kunal Talwar, and Li Zhang. "The geometry of differential privacy: the sparse and approximate cases." In Proceedings of the forty-fifth annual ACM symposium on Theory of computing, pp. 351-360. 2013.
