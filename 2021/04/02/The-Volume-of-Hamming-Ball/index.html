Entropy

We rely on sub-additivity of entropy.

Let XXX and YYY be discrete random variables, then

  1. H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y)H( X, Y ) \le H(X) + H(Y)H(X,Y)≤H(X)+H(Y).
  2. H(X∣Y)≤H(X)H(X | Y) \le H(X)H(X∣Y)≤H(X).

Proof.
Consider the ball B(1n,r)\mathcal{B}(1^n, r)B(1n,r), and a uniform random point X=(X1,...,Xn)X = (X_1, ..., X_n)X=(X1​,...,Xn​) in the ball B(1n,r)\mathcal{B}(1^n, r)B(1n,r). Its entropy is

H(X)=log⁡Vol(r). H(X) = \log \mathbb{Vol}(r). H(X)=logVol(r).

We write Y=X1Y = X_1Y=X1​. By sub-additivity of entropy and by symmetry,

H(X)≤H(X1)+H(X2)+...+H(Xn)=nH(Y). \begin{aligned} H(X) &\le H(X_1) + H(X_2) + ... + H(X_n) &= n H(Y). \end{aligned} H(X)​≤H(X1​)+H(X2​)+...+H(Xn​)​=nH(Y).​

Let ϵ≐Pr⁡[Y≠1]\epsilon \doteq \Pr[ Y \neq 1]ϵ≐Pr[Y=1] be the probability that YYY is not 111.
Further, by symmetry, Pr⁡[Y=i]\Pr[Y = i]Pr[Y=i] has equal probability for each i≠1i \neq 1i=1.
Hence,

H(Y)=Pr⁡[Y=1]⋅log⁡(1Pr⁡[Y=1])+∑i≠1Pr⁡[Y=i]⋅log⁡(1Pr⁡[Y=i])=(1−ϵ)⋅log⁡(11−ϵ)+ϵ⋅log⁡(q−1ϵ) \begin{aligned} H(Y) &= \Pr[ Y = 1] \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{\Pr[ Y = 1]}\right) + \sum_{i \neq 1} \Pr[ Y = i ] \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{\Pr[ Y = i]}\right) \\ % &= (1 - \epsilon) \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{1 - \epsilon} \right) + \epsilon \cdot \sum_{i \neq 1} \frac{ \Pr[ Y = i ] }{ \epsilon } \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{\Pr[ Y = i]}\right) \\ % &\le (1 - \epsilon) \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{1 - \epsilon} \right) + \epsilon \cdot \log \left( \sum_{i \neq 1} \frac{ \Pr[ Y = i ] }{ \epsilon } \frac{1}{\Pr[ Y = i]}\right) \\ &= (1 - \epsilon) \cdot \log \left( \frac{1}{1 - \epsilon} \right) + \epsilon \cdot \log \left( \frac{ q - 1 }{ \epsilon } \right) \\ \end{aligned} H(Y)​=Pr[Y=1]⋅log(Pr[Y=1]1​)+i=1∑​Pr[Y=i]⋅log(Pr[Y=i]1​)=(1−ϵ)⋅log(1−ϵ1​)+ϵ⋅log(ϵq−1​)​
