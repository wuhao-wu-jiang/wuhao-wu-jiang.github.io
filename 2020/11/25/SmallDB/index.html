Exponential Mechanism

One remedy for the above problem is that, for a set S⊂XmS \subset \mathcal{X}^mS⊂Xm, even if ∃f∈Q\exists f \in Q∃f∈Q, s.t., ∣f(S)−f(U)∣>λ|f(S) - f(U)| > \lambda∣f(S)−f(U)∣>λ, we output SSS as the representative of UUU with some probability, because SSS might be a feasible solution for UUU's neighbors. This motivates the exponential mechanism. In particular,

  1. It outputs the S∈XmS \in \mathcal{X}^mS∈Xm that minimizes max⁡f∈Q∣f(S)−f(U)∣\max_{f \in Q} |f(S) - f(U)|maxf∈Q​∣f(S)−f(U)∣ with highest probability.
  2. It outputs other S∈XmS \in \mathcal{X}^mS∈Xm with probability exponentially and inversely proportional to its error max⁡f∈Q∣f(S)−f(U)∣\max_{f \in Q} | f(S) - f(U) |maxf∈Q​∣f(S)−f(U)∣.

  1. For S∈XmS \in \mathcal{X}^mS∈Xm, define weight w(U,S)≐exp⁡(−ϵnmax⁡f∈Q∣f(S)−f(U)∣)w(U, S) \doteq \exp(- \epsilon n \max_{f \in Q} | f(S) - f(U) | )w(U,S)≐exp(−ϵnmaxf∈Q​∣f(S)−f(U)∣).
