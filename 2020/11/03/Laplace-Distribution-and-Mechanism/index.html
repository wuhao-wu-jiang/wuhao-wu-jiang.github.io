Posted on 2020-11-03 Edited on 2022-01-15

The density function of a Laplace distribution (denoted as Laplace(μ,b\mu, bμ,b) ) with location and scale parameters μ\muμ and bbb is given by

p(x)=12bexp⁡(−∣x−μ∣b)p(x) = \frac{1}{2 b} \exp \left( -\frac{ | x - \mu | }{ b } \right) p(x)=2b1​exp(−b∣x−μ∣​)

Laplacian Mechanism

Let (X,d)(\mathcal{X}, d)(X,d) be a metric space and f:X→Rnf: \mathcal{X} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^nf:X→Rn a function defined on X\mathcal{X}X.

Definition. x,x′∈Xx, x' \in \mathcal{X}x,x′∈X are called neighboring points if d(x,x′)=1d(x, x') = 1d(x,x′)=1.

Definition. The sensitivity Δ\DeltaΔ of fff is defined as

Δ≐max⁡x,x′∈X,d(x,x′)=1∥f(x)−f(x′)∥1 \Delta \doteq \max_{x, x' \in \mathcal{X}, d(x, x') = 1} \Vert f(x) - f(x') \Vert_1 Δ≐x,x′∈X,d(x,x′)=1max​∥f(x)−f(x′)∥1​

where ∥⋅∥1\Vert \cdot \Vert_1∥⋅∥1​ is the ℓ1\ell_1ℓ1​ distance. In other words, Δ\DeltaΔ is the maximum ℓ1\ell_1ℓ1​ distance between the images of two neighboring points.

We now construct a new randomized function, called Laplacian mechanism from fff, as follows.

Definition. The Laplacian mechanism of fff is defined as

g(x)=f(x)+Y g(x) = f(x) + Y g(x)=f(x)+Y

where Y=(Y1,Y2,...,Yn)Y = (Y_1, Y_2, ..., Y_n)Y=(Y1​,Y2​,...,Yn​) is a random vector with independent random variables Yi∼Laplace(Δ/ϵ)Y_i \sim Laplace(\Delta / \epsilon)Yi​∼Laplace(Δ/ϵ).
