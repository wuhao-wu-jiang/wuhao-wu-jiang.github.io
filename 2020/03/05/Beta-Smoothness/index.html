Characterizations of Beta-Smoothness

Posted on 2020-03-05 Edited on 2020-03-06

Definition

A differential function f:Rn→Rf: \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}f:Rn→R is β\betaβ-smooth if

∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥≤β∥y−x∥\|\nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \| \le \beta \|y - x\| ∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥≤β∥y−x∥

where ∥⋅∥\|\cdot \|∥⋅∥ is ℓ2\ell_2ℓ2​ norm.

Theorem 1.

A twice continuously differential function f:Rn→Rf : \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}f:Rn→R is β\betaβ-smooth if and only if

vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2v^T \nabla^2 f(z) v \le \beta \|v\|^2 vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2

for any z,v∈Rnz, v \in \mathbb{R}^nz,v∈Rn.

Proof.

  1. (ONLY IF). vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2v^T \nabla^2 f(z) v \le \beta \|v\|^2vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2 is equivalent to that the maximum eigenvalue of ∇2f(z)\nabla^2 f(z)∇2f(z) is at most β\betaβ. Let vvv be the eigenvector of ∇2f(z)\nabla^2 f(z)∇2f(z). By mean value theorem

    ∂f∂xi(z+v)−∂f∂xi(z)=∑j=1n(∂f∂xi∂xj(c))vj \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} (z + v) - \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} (z) = \sum_{j = 1}^n \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j} (c) \right) v_j ∂xi​∂f​(z+v)−∂xi​∂f​(z)=j=1∑n​(∂xi​∂xj​∂f​(c))vj​

    For some ccc between zzz and z+vz + vz+v. As fff is twice continuously differentiable, for any given ϵ>0\epsilon > 0ϵ>0, ∃v\exists v∃v that is small enough, such that

    ∣∂f∂xi(z+v)−∂f∂xi(z)∣∈∣∑j=1n(∂f∂xi∂xj(z))vj∣±ϵn\left| \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} (z + v) - \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i} (z) \right| \in \left| \sum_{j = 1}^n \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j} (z) \right) v_j \right| \pm \frac{\epsilon}{n} ∣∣∣∣∣​∂xi​∂f​(z+v)−∂xi​∂f​(z)∣∣∣∣∣​∈∣∣∣∣∣∣​j=1∑n​(∂xi​∂xj​∂f​(z))vj​∣∣∣∣∣∣​±nϵ​

    Hence,

    ∣∇f(z+v)−∇f(z)∣∈∣∇2f(z)v∣+ϵ| \nabla f(z + v) - \nabla f(z) | \in |\nabla^2 f(z) v| + \epsilon ∣∇f(z+v)−∇f(z)∣∈∣∇2f(z)v∣+ϵ

    By equivalence of ℓ1\ell_1ℓ1​-norm and ℓ2\ell_2ℓ2​ norm, we get for vvv small enough,

    ∥∇f(z+v)−∇f(z)∥∈∥∇2f(z)v∥+ϵ\| \nabla f(z + v) - \nabla f(z) \| \in \| \nabla^2 f(z) v \| + \epsilon ∥∇f(z+v)−∇f(z)∥∈∥∇2f(z)v∥+ϵ

    As ϵ\epsilonϵ can be arbitrary small, it concludes that

    ∥∇2f(z)v∥≤β∥v∥\| \nabla^2 f(z) v \| \le \beta \|v \| ∥∇2f(z)v∥≤β∥v∥

    which is equivalent to that the eigenvalues of ∇2f(z)\nabla^2 f(z)∇2f(z) is at most β\betaβ.

  2. (IF). We prove a stronger result that

    ∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥⋅∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥≤β(∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x) \| \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \| \cdot \| \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \| \le \beta \left( \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \right)^T (y - x) ∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥⋅∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥≤β(∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x)

    (∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x)≤∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥⋅∥y−x∥\left( \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \right)^T (y - x) \le \| \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \| \cdot \| y - x \| (∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x)≤∥∇f(y)−∇f(x)∥⋅∥y−x∥

    which will finish our proof.

    By Taylor series expansion, we have

    f(y)=f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+12(y−x)T∇2f(z)(y−x)≤f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+12β∥y−x∥2\begin{aligned} f(y) & = f(x) + \nabla f(x)^T (y - x) + \frac{1}{2} (y - x)^T \nabla^2 f(z) (y - x) \\ &\le f(x) + \nabla f(x)^T (y - x) + \frac{1}{2} \beta \| y - x \|^2 \end{aligned} f(y)​=f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+21​(y−x)T∇2f(z)(y−x)≤f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+21​β∥y−x∥2​

    for some zzz between xxx and yyy. The inequality holds since vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2v^T \nabla^2 f(z) v \le \beta \|v\|^2vT∇2f(z)v≤β∥v∥2 for any v∈Rnv \in \mathbb{R}^nv∈Rn.

    f(x)≤f(y)+∇f(y)T(x−y)+12β∥y−x∥2\begin{aligned} f(x) &\le f(y) + \nabla f(y)^T (x - y) + \frac{1}{2} \beta \| y - x \|^2 \end{aligned} f(x)​≤f(y)+∇f(y)T(x−y)+21​β∥y−x∥2​

    (∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x)T≤β∥y−x∥2\left( \nabla f(y) - \nabla f(x) \right)^T (y - x)^T \le \beta \| y - x \|^2 (∇f(y)−∇f(x))T(y−x)T≤β∥y−x∥2

□\square□

Theorem 2.

If fff is β\betaβ-smooth, then for x,y∈Rnx, y \in \mathbb{R}^nx,y∈Rn, we have

f(y)≤f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+12β∥y−x∥2f(y) \le f(x) + \nabla f(x)^T (y - x) + \frac{1}{2} \beta \| y - x \|^2 f(y)≤f(x)+∇f(x)T(y−x)+21​β∥y−x∥2
