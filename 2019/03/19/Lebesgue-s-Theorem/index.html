Theorem Lebesgue

If a function fff is monotone on a interval (a,b)(a, b)(a,b), then it is differentiable almost everywhere.

Proof:

E=∪α,β∈Q{x∈(a,b):Dˉf(x)≥α>β>D‾f(x)}E = \cup_{\alpha, \beta \in Q} \{ x \in (a, b) : \bar D f(x) \ge \alpha > \beta > \underline D f(x) \} E=∪α,β∈Q​{x∈(a,b):Dˉf(x)≥α>β>D​f(x)}
