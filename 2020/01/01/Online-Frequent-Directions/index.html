Online Frequent Directions

Posted on 2020-01-01 Edited on 2024-08-27

Problem

One scenario is to approximate A∈Rn×dA \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}A∈Rn×d in an online fashion, i.e., the rows of AAA comes on by one (no missing values).
Denote the iii-th row of AAA as aia_iai​.
We need to maintain a smaller matrix B∈Rℓ×dB \in \mathbb{R}^{\ell \times d}B∈Rℓ×d (ℓ≪n\ell \ll nℓ≪n), termed the matrix sketch of AAA, trying to minimize

max⁡x∈Rd,∥x∥2=1∣∥Ax∥22−∥Bx∥22∣=max⁡x∈Rd,∥x∥2=1∣xT(ATA−BTB)x∣=∥ATA−BTB∥2. \begin{aligned} \max_{x \in \mathbb{R}^d, \Vert x \Vert_2 = 1} \Big| \Vert A x \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B x \Vert_2^2 \Big| &= \max_{x \in \mathbb{R}^d, \Vert x \Vert_2 = 1} \Big| x^T (A^T A - B^T B) x \Big| = \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2. \end{aligned} x∈Rd,∥x∥2​=1max​∣∣∣∣​∥Ax∥22​−∥Bx∥22​∣∣∣∣​​=x∈Rd,∥x∥2​=1max​∣∣∣∣​xT(ATA−BTB)x∣∣∣∣​=∥ATA−BTB∥2​.​

Algorithm

We will present an algorithm, which achieves an error of

∥ATA−BTB∥2≤min⁡k∈[ℓ]∥A−A[k]∥F2ℓ−k, \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 \le \min_{k \in [\ell]} \frac{\Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert _F^2}{ \ell - k }, ∥ATA−BTB∥2​≤k∈[ℓ]min​ℓ−k∥A−A[k]​∥F2​​,

where ∥A∥F≐∑i∈[n],j∈[d]Ai,j2\Vert A \Vert _F \doteq \sqrt{ \sum_{ i \in [n], j \in [d] } A_{i,j}^2 }∥A∥F​≐∑i∈[n],j∈[d]​Ai,j2​​ is the Frobenius norm of AAA.

Algorithm [1]

  1. B←B \leftarrowB← empty matrix.
  2. For i∈[n]i \in [n]i∈[n]:
  3. \qquad B←[B;ai]B \leftarrow [B ; a_i]B←[B;ai​] matrix obtained by inserting aia_iai​ after the last row BBB
  4. \qquad BBB has ℓ\ellℓ rows:
  5. \qquad \qquad Ui,Σi,Vi←SVD(B)U_i, \Sigma_i, V_i \leftarrow \text{SVD} (B)Ui​,Σi​,Vi​←SVD(B), such that B=UiΣiViTB = U_i \Sigma_i V_i^TB=Ui​Σi​ViT​.
  6. \qquad \qquad Σi‾←max⁡{Σi2−σi,ℓ2I,0}\overline{\Sigma_i} \leftarrow \sqrt{ \max \{ \Sigma_i^2 - \sigma_{i, \ell}^2 I, 0 \} }Σi​​←max{Σi2​−σi,ℓ2​I,0}​
  7. \qquad \qquad B←Σi‾ViTB \leftarrow \overline{\Sigma_i} V_i^TB←Σi​​ViT​

Here σi,ℓ\sigma_{i, \ell}σi,ℓ​ is the ℓ\ellℓ-th largest singular value of Σi\Sigma_iΣi​.
The line 6 throws away all singular values that are less than σi,ℓ\sigma_{i, \ell}σi,ℓ​, and then subtract the squares of all singular values that are greater by σi,ℓ2\sigma_{i, \ell}^2σi,ℓ2​.

For the convenience of discussion, at each iteration, if BBB has less than ℓ\ellℓ rows after insertion, we still define

Ui,Σi,Vi←SVD(B),  and  Σi‾=Σi. U_i, \Sigma_i, V_i \leftarrow \text{SVD} (B) , \, \text{ and } \, \overline{\Sigma_i} = \Sigma_i. Ui​,Σi​,Vi​←SVD(B), and Σi​​=Σi​.

To analyze the error, first observe that, when the algorithm stops,

ATA−BTB=∑i∈[n](AiTAi+Bi−1TBi−1−BiTBi). \begin{aligned} A^T A - B^T B &= \sum_{i \in [n]} \left( A_i^T A_i + B_{i - 1}^T B_{i - 1} - B_i^T B_i \right). \end{aligned} ATA−BTB​=i∈[n]∑​(AiT​Ai​+Bi−1T​Bi−1​−BiT​Bi​).​

Therefore, for an arbitrary unit vector x∈Rdx \in \R^dx∈Rd,

xTATAx−xTBTBx=∑i∈[n](xTAiTAix+xTBi−1TBi−1x−xTBiTBix)=∑i=1j(⟨Ai,x⟩2+∥Bi−1x∥22−∥Bix∥22)=∑i=1n(∥ΣiViTv∥22−∥Σi‾ViTv∥22)≤∑i=1nσi,ℓ2. \begin{aligned} x^T A^T A x - x ^T B^T B x &= \sum_{i \in [n]} \left( x^T A_i^T A_i x + x^T B_{i - 1}^T B_{i - 1} x - x^T B_i^T B_i x \right) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^j \left( \left\langle A_i, x \right\rangle ^2 + \Vert B_{i - 1} x \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B_{i} x \Vert_2^2 \right) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n \left( \Vert \Sigma_i V_i^T v \Vert_2^2 - \Vert \overline{\Sigma_i} V_i^T v \Vert_2^2 \right) \\ &\le \sum_{i = 1}^n \sigma_{i, \ell }^2. \end{aligned} xTATAx−xTBTBx​=i∈[n]∑​(xTAiT​Ai​x+xTBi−1T​Bi−1​x−xTBiT​Bi​x)=i=1∑j​(⟨Ai​,x⟩2+∥Bi−1​x∥22​−∥Bi​x∥22​)=i=1∑n​(∥Σi​ViT​v∥22​−∥Σi​​ViT​v∥22​)≤i=1∑n​σi,ℓ2​.​

The last inequality holds since ViTxV_i^T xViT​x is a unit vector, and ΣiTΣi−Σi‾TΣi‾\Sigma_i^T \Sigma_i - \overline{\Sigma_i}^T \overline{\Sigma_i}ΣiT​Σi​−Σi​​TΣi​​ is a diagonal matrix whose elements are bounded by σi,ℓ2\sigma_{i, \ell}^2σi,ℓ2​.

It is left to bound the size of ∑i=1nσi,ℓ2\sum_{i = 1}^n \sigma_{i, \ell }^2∑i=1n​σi,ℓ2​:

ℓ⋅∑i=1nσi,ℓ2≤∑i=1n(∥Σi∥F2−∥Σi‾∥F2)=∑i=1n(∥Ci∥F2−∥Bi∥F2)=∑i=1n(∥ai∥22+∥Bi−1∥F2−∥Bi∥F2)=∥A∥F2−∥B∥F2.\begin{aligned} \ell \cdot \sum_{i = 1}^n \sigma_{i, \ell }^2 &\le \sum_{i = 1}^n \left( \Vert \Sigma_i \Vert^2_F - \Vert \overline{\Sigma_i} \Vert^2 _F \right) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n \left( \Vert C_i \Vert_F^2 - \Vert B_{i} \Vert^2_F \right) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n \left( \Vert a_i \Vert_2^2 + \Vert B_{i - 1} \Vert^2_F - \Vert B_{i} \Vert^2_F \right) \\ &= \Vert A \Vert^2_F - \Vert B \Vert^2_F. \end{aligned} ℓ⋅i=1∑n​σi,ℓ2​​≤i=1∑n​(∥Σi​∥F2​−∥Σi​​∥F2​)=i=1∑n​(∥Ci​∥F2​−∥Bi​∥F2​)=i=1∑n​(∥ai​∥22​+∥Bi−1​∥F2​−∥Bi​∥F2​)=∥A∥F2​−∥B∥F2​.​

Finally, let u1,…,ud∈Rdu_1, \ldots, u_d \in \mathbb{R}^du1​,…,ud​∈Rd be the right singular vectors of AAA.
Then for each k∈[ℓ]k \in [\ell]k∈[ℓ], it holds that

∥A∥F2−∥B∥F2=∑i=1k∥Aui∥22+∑i=k+1d∥Aui∥22−∥B∥F2=∑i=1k∥Aui∥22+∥A−A[k]∥22−∥B∥F2≤∑i=1k(∥Aui∥22−∥Bui∥22)+∥A−A[k]∥22∥B∥F2=∥BV∥F2≥∑i=1k∥Bui∥22≤∑i=1k(∥Aui∥22−∥Bui∥22)+∥A−A[k]∥22≤k⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2+∥A−A[k]∥22.\begin{aligned} \Vert A \Vert^2_F - \Vert B \Vert^2_F &= \sum_{i = 1}^k \Vert A u_i \Vert_2^2 + \sum_{i = k + 1}^d \Vert A u_i \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B \Vert^2_F \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^k \Vert A u_i \Vert_2^2 + \Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B \Vert^2_F \\ &\le \sum_{i = 1}^k \big( \Vert A u_i \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B u_i \Vert_2^2 \big) + \Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert_2^2 & \qquad \Vert B \Vert^2_F = \Vert B V\Vert^2_F \ge \sum_{i = 1}^k \Vert B u_i \Vert_2^2 \\ &\le \sum_{i = 1}^k \big( \Vert A u_i \Vert_2^2 - \Vert B u_i \Vert_2^2 \big) + \Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert_2^2 \\ &\le k \cdot \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 + \Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert_2^2. \end{aligned} ∥A∥F2​−∥B∥F2​​=i=1∑k​∥Aui​∥22​+i=k+1∑d​∥Aui​∥22​−∥B∥F2​=i=1∑k​∥Aui​∥22​+∥A−A[k]​∥22​−∥B∥F2​≤i=1∑k​(∥Aui​∥22​−∥Bui​∥22​)+∥A−A[k]​∥22​≤i=1∑k​(∥Aui​∥22​−∥Bui​∥22​)+∥A−A[k]​∥22​≤k⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2​+∥A−A[k]​∥22​.​∥B∥F2​=∥BV∥F2​≥i=1∑k​∥Bui​∥22​​

In conclusion, we have

ℓ⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2≤k⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2+∥A−A[k]∥22, \ell \cdot \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 \le k \cdot \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 + \Vert A - A_{[k]} \Vert_2^2, ℓ⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2​≤k⋅∥ATA−BTB∥2​+∥A−A[k]​∥22​,

which proves the theorem.

Remark: In the proof we apply the fact that the squared Frobenius norm of a matrix MMM equals the sum of the squares of its singular values.
Let M=∑j=1dσjujvj⊤M = \sum_{j = 1}^d \sigma_j u_j v_j^\topM=∑j=1d​σj​uj​vj⊤​, then

\begin{align} \Vert M \Vert_{\mathrm{F}}^2 = \Vert U \Sigma V^T \Vert_{\mathrm{F}}^2 = \Vert \Sigma \Vert_{\mathrm{F}}^2. \end{align}

The last inequality holds since UUU and VTV^TVT are rotations which does not change the Frobenius norm.

In conclusion,

∥ATA−BTB∥2∥A∥F2≤∥ATA−BTB∥2∥A∥F2−∥B∥F2≤2k.\begin{aligned} \frac{ \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 }{ \Vert A \Vert^2_F} \le \frac{ \Vert A^T A - B^T B \Vert_2 }{ \Vert A \Vert^2_F - \Vert B \Vert^2_F} \le \frac{2}{k}. \end{aligned} ∥A∥F2​∥ATA−BTB∥2​​≤∥A∥F2​−∥B∥F2​∥ATA−BTB∥2​​≤k2​.​

A Counterpart For Heavy Hitters

We apply similar idea for the heavy hitter problem in streaming model.

Problem

There is a streaming S={a1,...,am}S = \{ a_1, ..., a_m \}S={a1​,...,am​} of mmm elements each from a finite domain [d][d][d]. Given a parameter kkk, we want to find the elements in [d][d][d] whose frequencies, i.e., the number of times they appear in SSS, are at least kkk.

Algorithm

The following algorithm will provide under-estimates of elements’ frequencies, with space usage 2k2k2k and error m/km / km/k.

Processing Stream

  1. B0←B_0 \leftarrowB0​← an empty (element, frequency) list of capacity 2k2k2k.
  2. For t∈[m]t \in [m]t∈[m]:
  3. \qquad Ct←C_t \leftarrowCt​← list obtained by inserting {at,1}\{ a_t, 1 \}{at​,1} into Bt−1B_{t - 1}Bt−1​.
  4. \qquad If CtC_tCt​ has size 2k2k2k:
  5. \qquad \qquad Bt←B_t \leftarrowBt​← list obtained from CtC_tCt​ by merging frequencies.
  6. \qquad \qquad // each element in [d][d][d] now appears in BtB_tBt​ at most once.
  7. \qquad \qquad σt←\sigma_t \leftarrowσt​← the kkk-th largest frequency in BtB_tBt​.
  8. \qquad \qquad Subtract the frequency of each element in BtB_tBt​ by σt\sigma_tσt​.
  9. \qquad \qquad Remove elements with non-positive frequencies.
  10. \qquad Else
  11. \qquad \qquad σt←0;\sigma_t \leftarrow 0;σt​←0;
  12. \qquad \qquad Bt←CtB_t \leftarrow C_tBt​←Ct​.

QUERY
Input: BtB_tBt​ for some t∈[m]t \in [m]t∈[m], v∈[d].v \in [d].v∈[d].
Output: frequency estimation of vvv in {a1,....,at}\{a_1, ...., a_t \}{a1​,....,at​}

  1. f^Bt[v]←0;\hat f_{B_t}[v] \leftarrow 0;f^​Bt​​[v]←0;
  2. for (vi,fi)∈Bt(v_i, f_i) \in B_t(vi​,fi​)∈Bt​ do
  3. \qquad if vi=vv_i = vvi​=v then
  4. \qquad \qquad f^Bt[v]←f^Bt[v]+fi;\hat f_{B_t} [ v ] \leftarrow \hat f_{B_t} [v] + f_i;f^​Bt​​[v]←f^​Bt​​[v]+fi​;
  5. Return f^Bt[v]\hat f_{B_t} [v]f^​Bt​​[v].

Observe that for v∈[d]v \in [d]v∈[d], if vvv does not appears in BtB_tBt​, then the algorithm returns 0.

Time

The amortized time for each update is O(1)O(1)O(1).

  1. Inserting {ai,1}\{a_i, 1\}{ai​,1} into Bi−1B_{i - 1}Bi−1​ takes O(1)O(1)O(1) time.
  2. Merging CiC_iCi​, subtracting elements’ frequencies by σi\sigma_iσi​ and removing non-positive elements take O(k)O(k)O(k) time. But this happens only every k/2k / 2k/2 updates. Hence the amortized time is O(1)O(1)O(1).

Error

Proof. Obviously true by how the algorithm works.
