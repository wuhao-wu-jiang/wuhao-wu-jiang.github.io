Posted on 2024-10-22 Edited on 2024-10-25

Let (Ω,F,P)(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})(Ω,F,P) be a probability space.

Definition (Conditional Probability of A Set).
Given A,B∈FA, B \in \mathcal{F}A,B∈F, if P(B)≠0\mathbb{P}(B) \neq 0P(B)=0, then the conditional probability of AAA on BBB is defined by

P[A∣B]≐P[A∩B]P[B]. \mathbb{P}[ A \mid B ] \doteq \frac{\mathbb{P}[A \cap B]}{\mathbb{P}[B]}. P[A∣B]≐P[B]P[A∩B]​.

In general, the conditional probability is defined as a random variable measurable over a sub σ\sigmaσ-field over F\mathcal{F}F.
We present first the definition, and then use examples to illustrate it.

Definition (Conditional Probability). Let G\mathcal{G}G be a σ\sigmaσ-field in F\mathcal{F}F, and A∈FA \in \mathcal{F}A∈F.

  1. P[A∣G]\mathbb{P}[ A \mid \mathcal{G} ]P[A∣G] is measurable G\mathcal{G}G and integrable.
  2. P[A∣G]\mathbb{P}[A \mid \mathcal{G}]P[A∣G] satisfies the functional equation

∫GP[A∣G] dP=P[A∩G],∀G∈G. \int_G \mathbb{P}[A \mid \mathcal{G}] \, d \mathbb{P} = \mathbb{P}[ A \cap G ], \quad \forall G \in \mathcal{G}. ∫G​P[A∣G]dP=P[A∩G],∀G∈G.

The following provides the definition of conditional probability with respect to a random variable.

Definition. The conditional probability of A∈FA \in \mathcal{F}A∈F given a random variable XXX is defined as P[A∣σ(X)]\mathbb{P}[ A \mid \sigma(X) ]P[A∣σ(X)] and is denoted P[A∣X]\mathbb{P}[A \mid X]P[A∣X], where σ(X)\sigma(X)σ(X) is the σ\sigmaσ-field generated by XXX.

Remark. The σ\sigmaσ-field σ(X)\sigma(X)σ(X) consists of the sets [ω:X(ω)∈H][\omega: X(\omega) \in H][ω:X(ω)∈H] for H∈R1H \in \mathcal{R}^1H∈R1.

Existence

It is easy to observe that if such a random variable exists and we alter its value on a set in G\mathcal{G}G of measure 0, the conditions will still hold.
The key challenge, however, is to prove the existence of such a random variable.

Theorem. P[A∣G]\mathbb{P}[A \mid \mathcal{G}]P[A∣G] exists.

Proof.
Define a measure μA\mu_AμA​ in G\mathcal{G}G by

μA(G)≐P[A∩G],∀G∈G. \mu_A (G) \doteq \mathbb{P}[ A \cap G ], \quad \forall G \in \mathcal{G}. μA​(G)≐P[A∩G],∀G∈G.

Then μA\mu_AμA​ is absolutely continuous w.r.t. μ\muμ.
The Randon-Nikodym theorem implies that there exists a function f:Ω→Rf: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}f:Ω→R, measurable G\mathcal{G}G and integrable w.r.t. P\mathbb{P}P, s.t.,

□\square□
