Morris Counter

Posted on 2021-02-07

Problem

Given a stream \(S\) of \(n\) elements, a serve that observes the elements in \(S\) on by one is required to estimate the cardinality \(n\) with less than \(\log n\) bits, with relative error \(\epsilon \in (0, 1)\) and failure probability \(\delta\).

Let \(w \in (1, 2)\) be some parameter to be determined.

Algorithm. Morris Counter
1. \(k \leftarrow 0\).

  1. For \(i \leftarrow 1\) to \(n\):

  2. \(\qquad\) Increase \(k\) by \(1\), with probability \(w^{ - k}\).

  3. \(\qquad X_i \leftarrow \frac{w^k - 1}{ w - 1}\);

  4. Output \(X_n\).

Next we discuss the properties of the algorithm.

Theorem 1. \(\forall t \in [n]\), \(\mathbb{E} [ X_t ] = t\).

Proof: Define \(X_0 = 0\). Hence, \(\mathbb{E} [ X_0 ] = 0\). For \(t \ge 1\), we prove the claim by induction.

For \(k \ge 0\), denote \(v_k \doteq \frac{w^k - 1}{ w - 1}\). Conditioned on \(X_{t - 1} = v_k\) for some \(k \ge 0\), it holds that \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E} [ X_t | X_{t - 1} = v_k ] &= w^{-k} \frac{w^{k + 1} - 1}{ w - 1} + (1 - w^{-k} ) \cdot v_k \\ &= w^{-k} \frac{w^{k + 1} - w^k + w^k - 1}{ w - 1} + (1 - w^{-k} ) \cdot v_k \\ &= w^{-k} \left( w^k + v_k \right) + (1 - w^{-k} ) \cdot v_k \\ &= v_k + 1 \\ &= X_{t - 1} + 1, \end{aligned} \]

Assuming that \(\mathbb{E} [X_{t - 1} ] = t - 1\), we have \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E} [ X_t ] = \mathbb{E} [ \mathbb{E} [ X_t | X_{t - 1} ] ] = \mathbb{E} [X_{t - 1} ] + 1 = t. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

Theorem 2. \(\forall t \in [n]\), \(\mathbb{Var } [ X_t ] = (w - 1) \cdot \frac{ t^2 }{ 2 }\).

Proof:

Since \(w^k = (w - 1) \cdot v_k + 1\), we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E} [ X_t^2 | X_{t - 1} = v_k ] &= w^{-k} \left( \frac{w^{k + 1} - 1}{ w - 1} \right)^2 + (1 - w^{-k} ) \cdot ( v_k )^2 \\ &= w^{-k} \left( w^k + v_k \right)^2 + (1 - w^{-k} ) \cdot (v_k)^2 \\ &= w^k + 2 \cdot v_k + v_k^2 \\ &= (w + 1) \cdot v_k + 1 + v_k^2 \end{aligned} \]

Therefore, since \(X_0 = 0\), \[ \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E} [ X_t^2 ] &= \mathbb{E} [ \mathbb{E} [ X_t^2 | X_{t - 1} = v_k ] ] \\ &= (w + 1) \cdot \mathbb{E} [ X_{t - 1} ] + 1 + \mathbb{E} [ X_{t - 1} ^2] \\ &= \mathbb{E} [ X_{t - 1}^2 ] + (w + 1) \cdot (t - 1) + 1 \\ &... \\ &= \mathbb{E} [ X_0^2 ] + (w + 1) \cdot \sum_{i = 1}^{t - 1} i + (t -1) \\ &= (w + 1) \cdot \frac{ t (t - 1) }{ 2 } + (t - 1) \\ &\le (w - 1) \cdot \frac{ t^2 }{ 2 } + t^2, \end{aligned} \]

and \[ \mathbb{Var } [ X_t ] = \mathbb{E} [ X_t^2 ] - (\mathbb{E} [ X_t ])^2 \le (w - 1) \cdot \frac{ t^2 }{ 2 } . \]

Corollary 3. By Chebyshev's inequality, \[ \Pr[ | X_t - t | \ge \epsilon \cdot t ] \le (w - 1) \cdot \frac{ t^2 }{ 2 \epsilon^2 \cdot t^2} = \frac{w - 1}{ 2 \epsilon^2 }. \]

Corollary 4. If we set \(w = 1 + 2 \epsilon^2 \delta\), then the failure probability is at most \(\delta\).

Reference.

[1] G. Cormode and K. Yi, Small Summaries for Big Data, 1st ed. Cambridge University Press, 2020.
