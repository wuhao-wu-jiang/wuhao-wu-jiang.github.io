Logarithm Inequalities

Posted on 2021-06-15 Edited on 2021-06-16

We study inequalities that play important roles in obtaining Chernoff bounds. These inequalities are related to the following function defined as \[ h(t) = (1 + t) \ln (1 + t) - t\,,\qquad \forall t > -1. \]

Note that \[ \begin{aligned} h'(t) &= \ln (1 + t) \,, \\ h^{ (2 + n) } (t) &= (-1)^n \cdot n! \cdot (1 + t)^{ - (n + 1) } \,, \qquad \forall n \in \mathbb{N} \end{aligned} \]

Hence, \[ \begin{aligned} h(0) = h'(0) = 0 \,, \text{ and } \quad h^{ (2 + n) } (0) = (-1)^n \cdot n!\,, \qquad \forall n \in \mathbb{N} \,. \end{aligned} \]

Inequalities

\(\diamondsuit\)

\[ h(t) \ge \frac{1}{1 + t} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} \]

Plot of \(h(t) - \frac{1}{1 + t} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2}\).

Proof.

Via Taylor's expansion, \(\exists c \in [0, t]\), s.t., \[ \begin{aligned} h(t) = h(0) + h'(0) \cdot t + h''(c) \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} = \frac{1}{1 + c} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} \ge \frac{1}{1 + t} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2}. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

\(\clubsuit\)

\[ h(t) \ge \frac{t^2}{2 + t} = \frac{1}{1 + t /2 } \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} \]

Plot of \(h(t) - \frac{1}{1 + t / 2} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2}\).

Proof. By convexity of \(f(x) \doteq 1 / (1 + tx)\), we have \[ \ln (1 + t) = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1 + tx} \, d\, (tx) \ge t \cdot \frac{1}{1 + \int_0^1 tx \, d\, x } = \frac{t}{1 + t / 2}. \]

It follows that \[ h(t) = (1 + t) \ln (1 + t) - t \ge \frac{t^2 + t - t - t^2 / 2}{1 + t / 2} = \frac{t^2 / 2}{1 + t / 2}. \]

\(\square\)

\(\spadesuit\)

\[ h(t) \ge \frac{t^2}{2 + (2 / 3) \cdot t} = \frac{1}{1 + t / 3} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} \]

Plot of \(h(t) - \frac{1}{1 + t / 3} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2}\).

Proof 1.

\[ \begin{aligned} h(t) - h(0) &= \int_0^1 h'( tx) \, d\, (tx) \\ &= \int_0^1 \left( h'(0) + \int_0^x h''( ty) \, d\, (ty) \right) d\, (tx) \\ &= h'(0) \cdot t + t^2 \int_0^1 \left( \int_0^x h''( ty) \, d\, y \right) d\, x \\ \end{aligned} \]

Substituting \(h(0) = h'(0) = 0\), and \(h''(ty) = 1 / (1 + ty)\), we get \[ \begin{aligned} h(t) &= t^2 \int_0^1 \int_0^x \frac{1}{1 + ty} \, d\, y \, d\, x . \end{aligned} \]

Let \(S \doteq \{ 0 \le x \le 1, 0 \le y \le x \}\). Its area, denoted as \(|S|\), equals \(1 / 2\).

Consider the convex function \(f(x, y) = 1 / (1 + ty)\) defined on \(S\). By Jensen's inequality, \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{1}{|S|} \cdot \int_0^1 \int_0^x \frac{1}{1 + ty} \, d\, y \, d\, x &\ge \frac{1}{1 + t \cdot \int_0^1 \int_0^x \frac{1}{|S|} \cdot y \, d\, y \, d\, x } = \frac{1}{1 + t / 3}. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

Proof 2. Define \[ \begin{aligned} f(t) &= h(t) - \frac{3}{t + 3} \cdot \frac{t^2}{2} \\ &= h(t) - \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{t^2 - 9 + 9}{t + 3} \\ &= h(t) - \frac{3}{2} \cdot \Big( t - 3 + \frac{9}{t + 3} \Big) \end{aligned} \]

Then \[ \begin{aligned} f'(t) &= \ln (1 + t) - \frac{3}{2} \cdot \Big( 1 - \frac{9}{(t + 3)^2} \Big) \,, \\ f''(t) &= \frac{1}{1 + t} - \frac{27}{ (t + 3)^3 } \\ &= \frac{ t^3 + 3 t^2 \cdot 3 + 3 t \cdot 9 + 27 - 27 - 27t }{ (1 + t) (t + 3)^3} \\ &= \frac{ t^3 + 9 t^2 }{ (1 + t) (t + 3)^3} \\ &\ge 0. \end{aligned} \]

Hence, for all \(t \ge 0\), \[ \begin{aligned} f'(t) &\ge f'(0) = 0 \,, \\ f(t) &\ge f(0) = 0. \end{aligned} \]

\(\square\)

Reference
