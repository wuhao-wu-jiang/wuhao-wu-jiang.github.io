Holder's Inequality

Posted on 2022-03-31 Edited on 2022-04-02

Let (Ω,F,P)(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mathbb{P})(Ω,F,P) be a probability space, and X,Y:Ω→RX, Y: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}X,Y:Ω→R be random variables.

Holder’s Inequality. Let p,q∈R+p, q \in \mathbb{R}^+p,q∈R+, such that 1/p+1/q=11 / p + 1 / q = 11/p+1/q=1.
Then

E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣]≤E1/p[∣X∣p]⋅E1/q[∣Y∣q] \mathbb{E}\left[ |X| \cdot |Y| \right] \le \mathbb{E}^{1 / p} \left[ |X|^p \right] \cdot \mathbb{E}^{1 / q} \left[ |Y|^q \right] E[∣X∣⋅∣Y∣]≤E1/p[∣X∣p]⋅E1/q[∣Y∣q]

Remark 1. If ∣X∣p|X|^p∣X∣p and ∣Y∣q|Y|^q∣Y∣q are integrable, so is ∣X∣⋅∣Y∣|X| \cdot |Y|∣X∣⋅∣Y∣.

Proof. The claim is trivial, if either E[∣X∣p]=∞\mathbb{E}[ |X|^p ] = \inftyE[∣X∣p]=∞ or E[∣Y∣q]=∞\mathbb{E}[ |Y|^q ] = \inftyE[∣Y∣q]=∞.

Therefore, we consider just the case when both ∣X∣p|X|^p∣X∣p and ∣Y∣q|Y|^q∣Y∣q are integrable.
By concavity of the function f(t)=ln⁡tf(t) = \ln tf(t)=lnt:

1pln⁡ap+1qln⁡bq≤ln⁡(app+bqq),∀a,b>0. \frac{1}{p} \ln a^p + \frac{1}{q} \ln b^q \le \ln \left( \frac{a^p}{p} + \frac{b^q}{q} \right), \qquad \forall a, b > 0. p1​lnap+q1​lnbq≤ln(pap​+qbq​),∀a,b>0.

We obtain the Young’s Inequality:

ab≤app+bqq,∀a,b>0. a b \le \frac{a^p}{p} + \frac{b^q}{q}, \qquad \forall a, b > 0. ab≤pap​+qbq​,∀a,b>0.

Let s=E1/p[∣X∣p]s = \mathbb{E}^{1 / p} \left[ |X|^p \right]s=E1/p[∣X∣p] and t=E1/q[∣Y∣q]t = \mathbb{E}^{1 / q} \left[ |Y|^q \right]t=E1/q[∣Y∣q].
Then for each ω∈Ω\omega \in \Omegaω∈Ω, it holds that

∣X(ω)∣s⋅∣Y(ω)∣t≤∣X(ω)∣pp⋅sp+∣Y(ω)∣pq⋅tq \frac{|X(\omega)|}{s} \cdot \frac{|Y(\omega)|}{t} \le \frac{|X(\omega)|^p}{p \cdot s^p} + \frac{|Y(\omega)|^p}{q \cdot t^q} s∣X(ω)∣​⋅t∣Y(ω)∣​≤p⋅sp∣X(ω)∣p​+q⋅tq∣Y(ω)∣p​

Define a new random variable

Z=∣X∣pp⋅sp+∣Y∣pq⋅tq. Z = \frac{|X|^p}{p \cdot s^p} + \frac{|Y|^p}{q \cdot t^q}. Z=p⋅sp∣X∣p​+q⋅tq∣Y∣p​.

By monotonicity and linearity of integration,

E[∣X∣s⋅∣Y∣t]≤E[∣X∣pp⋅sp]+E[∣Y∣pq⋅tq]=1. \mathbb{E}\left[ \frac{|X|}{s} \cdot \frac{|Y|}{t} \right] \le \mathbb{E}\left[ \frac{|X|^p}{p \cdot s^p} \right] + \mathbb{E}\left[ \frac{|Y|^p}{q \cdot t^q} \right] = 1. E[s∣X∣​⋅t∣Y∣​]≤E[p⋅sp∣X∣p​]+E[q⋅tq∣Y∣p​]=1.

□\square□

Heavy-Tailed Distribution

Let (Ω,F,Pr⁡)(\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \Pr)(Ω,F,Pr) be a probability space, and X:Ω→RX: \Omega \rightarrow \mathbb{R}X:Ω→R be a random variable.
Assume that there exists constants a∈(0,1]a \in (0, 1]a∈(0,1], b∈R+b \in \mathbb{R}^+b∈R+, such that

E[∣X∣1+a]≤b. \mathbb{E}\left[|X|^{1 + a}\right] \le b. E[∣X∣1+a]≤b.

Theorem. Let T∈R+T \in \mathbb{R}^+T∈R+. Then

E[∣X∣⋅I[∣X∣>T]]≤bTa. \mathbb{E}\left[|X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} \right] \le \frac{b}{ T^a}. E[∣X∣⋅I[∣X∣>T]​]≤Tab​.

Proof.

E[∣X∣1+a]=∫0∞Pr⁡[∣X∣1+a>r] dr=∫0∞Pr⁡[∣X∣1+a>s1+a] ds1+a=∫0∞(1+a)sa⋅Pr⁡[∣X∣>s] ds≥∫0T(1+a)sa⋅Pr⁡[∣X∣>T] ds+(1+a)⋅Ta⋅∫T∞Pr⁡[∣X∣>s] ds=T1+a⋅Pr⁡[∣X∣>T]+(1+a)⋅Ta⋅∫T∞Pr⁡[∣X∣>s] ds. \begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}\left[|X|^{1 + a}\right] &= \int_0^\infty \Pr\left[ |X|^{1 + a} > r \right] \, dr = \int_0^\infty \Pr\left[ |X|^{1 + a} > s^{1 + a} \right] \, ds^{1 + a} \\ &= \int_0^\infty (1 + a) s^a \cdot \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s \\ &\ge \int_0^T (1 + a) s^a \cdot \Pr\left[ |X| > T \right] \, d s + (1 + a) \cdot T^a \cdot \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s \\ &= T^{1 + a} \cdot \Pr[ |X| > T ] + (1 + a) \cdot T^a \cdot \int_T^\infty \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] \, d s. \end{aligned} E[∣X∣1+a]​=∫0∞​Pr[∣X∣1+a>r]dr=∫0∞​Pr[∣X∣1+a>s1+a]ds1+a=∫0∞​(1+a)sa⋅Pr[∣X∣>s]ds≥∫0T​(1+a)sa⋅Pr[∣X∣>T]ds+(1+a)⋅Ta⋅∫T∞​Pr[∣X∣>s]ds=T1+a⋅Pr[∣X∣>T]+(1+a)⋅Ta⋅∫T∞​Pr[∣X∣>s]ds.​

and for s>Ts > Ts>T,

Pr⁡[∣X∣⋅I[∣X∣>T]>s]=Pr⁡[{ω∈Ω:∣X(ω)∣>s∧∣X(ω)∣>T}]=Pr⁡[∣X∣>s]. \begin{aligned} \Pr\left[ |X| \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\left[ |X| > T \right]} > s \right] &= \Pr\left[ \left\{ \omega \in \Omega : |X(\omega)| > s \wedge |X(\omega)| > T \right\} \right] = \Pr\left[ |X| > s \right] . \end{aligned} Pr[∣X∣⋅I[∣X∣>T]​>s]​=Pr[{ω∈Ω:∣X(ω)∣>s∧∣X(ω)∣>T}]=Pr[∣X∣>s].​
