Birthday Paradox

Posted on 2018-08-26 Edited on 2022-12-07

Suppose that we throw mmm balls into nnn bins uniformly at random, where nnn is a fixed number. What is the probability that some pair of balls are placed in the same bin?

Define Xi,jX_{i, j}Xi,j​ be the indicator variable that the iii-th ball and the jjj-th ball go to the same bin. Its expectation is given by

E[Xi,j]=1⋅Pr⁡[Xi,j=1]+0⋅Pr⁡[Xi,j=0]=1n. \mathbb{E}\left[ X_{i,j} \right] = 1\cdot \Pr\left[ X_{i,j} = 1\right] + 0 \cdot \Pr\left[X_{i,j} = 0\right] = \frac{1}{n}. E[Xi,j​]=1⋅Pr[Xi,j​=1]+0⋅Pr[Xi,j​=0]=n1​.
